# United States Institute Of Peace

### Funding

**2024: Fitzpatrick Voted Against Reducing United States Institute Of
Peace Funding By \$16.4 Million.** In June 2024, Fitzpatrick voted
against , according to Congressional Quarterly, "amendment no. 3 that
would reduce by \$16.4 million funding for the United States Institute
of Peace." The vote was on the amendment. The underlying legislation was
the FY 2025 State-Foreign Operations Appropriations. The House rejected
the amendment by a vote of 164 to 244. \[House Vote 299,
[6/27/24](http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2024/roll299.xml); Congressional
Quarterly, [6/27/24](https://plus.cq.com/vote/2024/H/299?11);
Congressional Actions,
[H.Amdt.1032](https://www.congress.gov/amendment/118th-congress/house-amendment/1032/actions);
Congressional Actions, [H.R.
8771](http://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/house-bill/8771/all-actions)\]
