# International Telecommunication Union

### Security Campaign Director

**2022: Fitzpatrick Voted To Appoint A Security Campaign Director To
Promote Candidates For Elections At The International Telecommunication
Union Who Will Defend Democratic Principles, Internet Freedom And
Telecommunication Security.** In April 2022, according to Congressional
Quarterly, Fitzpatrick voted for the Transatlantic Telecommunications
Security Act, which would "direct the department to appoint a security
campaign director to lead efforts to promote candidates for elections at
the International Telecommunication Union who will defend democratic
values, internet freedom and telecommunication security." The vote was
on passage. The House passed the bill by a vote of 366-60, thus the bill
was sent to the Senate. \[House Vote 133,
[4/27/22](http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2022/roll133.xml); Congressional
Quarterly, [4/27/22](https://plus.cq.com/vote/2022/H/133?6);
Congressional Actions, [H.R.
3344](https://www.congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/house-bill/3344/actions)\]

-   **The Bill Would Require The Security Campaign Director To Report On
    Progress In Supporting Cross-Border Connectivity Of
    Telecommunication Infrastructure In Ukraine, Moldova, Georgia And
    Western Balkan Nations.** According to Congressional Quarterly, "It
    would also require the director to report to the department on
    progress in supporting cross-border connectivity of
    telecommunication infrastructure in Ukraine, Moldova, Georgia and
    the Western Balkan countries." \[Congressional Quarterly,
    [4/27/22](https://plus.cq.com/vote/2022/H/133?6)\]
