# Prison Sentences

### Crack Cocaine Sentence Reduction

**2022: Fitzpatrick Voted To Provide Low-Level Convicts Of Crack Cocaine
To Be Eligible For Retroactive Reduction Of Their Prison Sentences Under
The 2010 Sentencing Act.** In November 2022, according to Congressional
Quarterly, Fitzpatrick voted for the Terry Technical Correction Act,
which would "specify that individuals convicted of lower-level offenses
involving crack cocaine would be eligible for retroactive reduction of
their prison sentences under the 2010 Fair Sentencing Act." The vote was
on passage. The House passed the bill by a vote of 307-101. The Senate
did not take substantive action on the bill. \[House Vote 488,
[11/29/22](http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2022/roll488.xml); Congressional
Quarterly, [11/29/22](https://plus.cq.com/vote/2022/H/488?21);
Congressional Actions, [H.R.
5455](https://www.congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/house-bill/5455/all-actions)\]
