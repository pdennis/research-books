# Government Spending, Accountability, and Economic Policy

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson has consistently criticized the Biden administration’s economic and spending policies, arguing they are harmful and out of touch for Iowans and Americans ([Iowa Torch](https://iowatorch.com/2023/06/28/hinson-commonsense-vs-crazy-fighting-the-biden-administrations-out-of-touch-agenda/), [Daily Nonpareil](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64KF-H481-DXVP-V0H8-00000-00&context=1519360), [Globe Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64KF-H481-DXVP-V1N2-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson has supported targeted and responsible government financial relief during the pandemic, expressing strong backing for the Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) and direct payments to individuals ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:60ST-CC01-JBCN-40KC-00000-00&context=1519360), [Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:60SY-PXB1-DXVP-V2YH-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She emphasized the need for infrastructure plans to focus on traditional projects and broadband expansion, showing some willingness to work with Democrats on a compromise bill ([Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62N7-1P91-DYWP-80B4-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Despite this willingness to collaborate, Hinson ultimately voted against the federal infrastructure bill, citing concerns over federal spending and linkage with other Democratic priorities ([Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6439-PPW1-JCDM-J05M-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson has repeatedly cited inflation and rising costs as direct challenges resulting from the administration’s policies, highlighting the effect on fixed-income residents, families, and small businesses ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64KF-3RT1-JBCN-42XV-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Potential vulnerability: Hinson's vote against the major infrastructure bill has been criticized as contradicting her expressed support for some aspects of infrastructure investment ([Gazette letter to the editor](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:642P-M7T1-DXVP-V157-00000-00&context=1519360)).
