# Legislation and Congressional Actions on Judicial Affairs

### Summary

> * Rep. Ashley Hinson consistently opposed independent congressional commissions on the January 6 attack, arguing that such probes could interfere with ongoing law enforcement investigations by the FBI and Department of Justice; she said she might support commissions once those investigations are complete ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62RM-7981-DXVP-V3NT-00000-00&context=1519360), [Daily Nonpareil](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:63JX-BMJ1-DXVP-V173-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson supported and co-sponsored bipartisan efforts to investigate the Capitol attack through a national commission, while later opposing specific commission proposals due to concerns over investigative overlap ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:61SH-RYH1-DXVP-V39V-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She advocated for reforming the judicial branch, specifically supporting legislation to limit federal judges from issuing nationwide injunctions, which she described as “judicial overreach” by “partisan federal judges” ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FGR-HXX3-S8TT-W067-00000-00&context=1519360), [Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FGP-7403-SCKF-V0VT-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson emphasized the importance of coequal branches of government and due process, and planned meetings with judges to explore judicial reforms that would protect constitutional principles ([Des Moines Register](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FFN-BY13-RRT2-N26G-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She took a leading role in co-introducing legislation to establish a DOJ task force aimed at detecting, investigating, and prosecuting trade-related crimes, indicating a focus on justice system modernization ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D86-NTR1-JBCN-31XJ-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson voted for bipartisan legislation ending forced arbitration in cases of sexual assault and harassment and worked on Marsy's Law to improve victim notification processes, reflecting support for victims’ rights and accountability ([Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64SR-2CC1-DYWP-82CM-00000-00&context=1519360), [Quad-City Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5RP7-21R1-DXVP-V3GC-00000-00&context=1519360)).
