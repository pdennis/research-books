# Infrastructure, Energy, and Economic Innovation

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson played a leading role in securing $829 million in federal funding from the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act to modernize locks and dams on the Upper Mississippi River, which she publicly touted as "game-changing" for Iowa's agriculture, supply chain, and river communities ([PolitiFact](https://www.politifact.com/factchecks/2022/jan/21/liz-mathis/hinson-joined-letter-iowa-lock-and-dam-money-she-d/), [Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/iowa-gop-rep-ashley-hinson-slammed-touting-infrastructure-bill-she-voted-against-1671409)).
> * Despite advocating for and requesting targeted infrastructure investments, Hinson voted against the bipartisan infrastructure bill that funded these projects, later joining bipartisan letters to prioritize funding for river infrastructure ([PolitiFact](https://www.politifact.com/factchecks/2022/jan/21/liz-mathis/hinson-joined-letter-iowa-lock-and-dam-money-she-d/), [Daily Nonpareil](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64K7-N191-JBCN-442R-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * In recent years, Hinson has continued to request millions in federal funding for local projects, including airport improvements, wastewater treatment upgrades, drought information systems, and educational infrastructure in Iowa ([Sioux City Journal](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C6K-4SG1-DXVP-T1F2-00000-00&context=1519360), [Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C79-S251-DXVP-T40M-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson has emphasized the economic importance of upgraded infrastructure, citing the Mississippi River's role in grain exports and the need for modern, resilient systems to support Iowa's economy ([Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:635Y-1BN1-JCDM-J19W-00000-00&context=1519360), [Valley News Today](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64K7-MXY1-DXVP-V2HK-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson's votes and public statements have drawn criticism from opponents, who accuse her of "flip-flopping"—opposing the infrastructure bill while later taking credit for its investments benefiting her district ([Quad-City Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64K7-TMP1-JBCN-4178-00000-00&context=1519360), [Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64KF-3RT1-JBCN-42XV-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson has supported public-private partnerships, prioritized community feedback for project selection, and maintained a preference for "targeted" infrastructure spending focused on local and critical needs ([Daily Nonpareil](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62SR-CMY1-JBCN-4416-00000-00&context=1519360), [Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62V6-CKN1-JBCN-413Y-00000-00&context=1519360)).
