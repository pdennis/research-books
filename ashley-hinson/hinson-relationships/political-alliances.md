### Summary

> * Strong alignment with Donald Trump leaves Hinson vulnerable to criticism over extremism, support for the “Big Lie,” and prioritizing Trump’s agenda over constituent needs, especially after her public pledges to back Trump and campaign as his surrogate ([Des Moines Register](https://www.desmoinesregister.com/story/news/politics/elections/2024/11/05/iowa-election-results-ashley-hinson-sarah-corkery-jody-puffett-2nd-congressional-district/75721251007/), [Radio Iowa](https://www.radioiowa.com/2024/05/31/hinson-says-trumps-conviction-will-backfire-on-democrats/))

> * Opponents and editorial boards frequently highlight Hinson’s shift from moderate to strongly partisan, accusing her of divisive rhetoric, lack of cross-party cooperation, and being out of touch with broader Iowa voters ([Gazette editorial](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66NV-TJS1-DXVP-V0KD-00000-00&context=1519360), [Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:638J-NJ41-JBCN-435J-00000-00&context=1519360))
