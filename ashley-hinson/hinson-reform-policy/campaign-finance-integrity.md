# Campaign Finance and Election Integrity

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson has consistently opposed federal election reform measures such as H.R. 1, arguing that they would "federalize elections," overrule state authority, and introduce public financing and voucher systems she opposes ([Gazette, 3/5/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6250-9XM1-JBCN-44X3-00000-00&context=1519360); [Waterloo Courier, 3/5/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6250-5JD1-JBTP-H48C-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She has advocated for strong state authority over election administration, supporting laws that make it "easier to vote legally and harder to cheat," and opposed federal oversight or standardization, even declining to comment on specific pending state-level bills ([Quad-City Times, 3/5/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6251-4SY1-DXVP-V1MX-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson introduced and supported bipartisan legislation to ban former federal employees, including senior executive branch officials, from lobbying their former agencies, and has called for expanding these bans to former members of Congress and increasing transparency ([Telegraph Herald, 8/1/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CMM-NF61-DYWP-8037-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She supported recent efforts to prohibit noncitizens from voting in federal elections and has warned about potential vulnerabilities such as state-issued driver's licenses enabling possible unauthorized registration ([Gazette, 7/12/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D37-4K81-JBCN-33T2-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * While Hinson voted to certify the 2020 presidential election and condemned the January 6 attack, she ultimately voted against an independent commission to investigate the insurrection and against bipartisan election reform bills, which drew criticism from political opponents ([The Gazette, 7/10/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:65WW-9SR1-JBCN-426X-00000-00&context=1519360); [Muscatine Journal, 9/22/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66FX-4RY1-DXVP-V3KC-00000-00&context=1519360)).
