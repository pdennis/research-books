# Legislative Actions on Medicare

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson consistently opposed expanding Medicare to cover dental, hearing, and vision benefits, arguing for program sustainability over what she described as a "drastic expansion" and labeling some proposals as "socialism" ([Iowa Starting Line](https://iowastartingline.com/2021/09/27/mathis-criticizes-hinson-for-medicare-expansion-socialism-comments/), [Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:63PW-0911-DYWP-801W-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson rejected measures allowing Medicare to negotiate drug prices and voted against capping out-of-pocket insulin costs at $35, instead supporting increased competition and transparency to lower drug costs ([Sioux City Journal](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:65WX-4YT1-JBCN-43H6-00000-00&context=1519360), [The Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:65WW-9SR1-JBCN-426X-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She endorsed alternatives like House Resolution 19 and the House GOP "Lower Cost, More Cures Act," which would cap Medicare Part D out-of-pocket insulin costs and support measures to increase drug price competition ([The Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:63JV-PWM1-JBCN-4415-00000-00&context=1519360), [Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:666F-G0X1-JBCN-447J-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * In 2023, Hinson co-sponsored and supported bipartisan legislation to adjust Medicare payments for medical equipment and prosthetics, aiming to address affordability for recipients ([Sioux City Journal](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:697J-N791-DXVP-T3VS-00000-00&context=1519360), [Quad-City Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:697N-BGB1-DXVP-T1D7-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson consistently emphasized the need to maintain Medicare's and Social Security’s solvency, expressing opposition to raising the retirement age or cutting benefits, and calling for bipartisan solutions and efforts to reduce waste and fraud ([Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:65C4-MYR1-DYWP-803G-00000-00&context=1519360), [Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D6V-0DS1-JBCN-31CB-00000-00&context=1519360)).
