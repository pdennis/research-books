# Healthcare and Public Health

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson has emphasized the critical importance of ensuring healthcare access in rural Iowa, highlighting facilities that allow residents to receive care close to home rather than traveling to larger cities ([Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier, 8/9/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CP9-YMJ1-JBCN-310T-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson's office is committed to supporting Medicaid for those who "truly need it" and asserts Medicaid must be sustainable for the long term ([Gazette, 3/7/25](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F90-GNM3-S2MP-53W3-00000-00&context=1519360); [Sioux City Journal, 3/7/25](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F91-9M63-RTCK-R4WK-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson's office disputed Congressional Budget Office (CBO) analyses suggesting negative impacts from budget proposals, instead citing the House Committee on Ways & Means support for extending tax cuts to benefit working families and small businesses ([Sioux City Journal, 3/7/25](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F91-9M63-RTCK-R4WK-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She has worked to expand access to maternal health care, address the infant formula shortage, and increase affordable child care options for Iowa families ([Globe Gazette, 10/9/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66K3-HY61-DXVP-V06W-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Potential vulnerability: By limiting Medicaid support to "those who truly need it," there may be concern about how need is defined and if some vulnerable populations could lose coverage.
