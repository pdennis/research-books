# Economic Security and Social Safety Net

### Summary

> * Hinson repeatedly voted against the American Rescue Plan Act and major pandemic relief measures, including stimulus checks, unemployment extensions, and enhanced child tax credits, leading to criticism that she opposed economic supports for families and businesses ([Gazette, 3/19/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:627Y-RV41-DXVP-V4H5-00000-00&context=1519360); [Telegraph Herald, 5/21/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62RB-RG11-DYWP-832K-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Critics, including local Democrats and letter writers, have accused Hinson of pushing for cuts or stricter eligibility to core safety net programs like Social Security, SNAP, veterans' benefits, and Medicaid, especially during debt ceiling and budget battles ([Gazette, 5/27/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68B5-9DP1-DXVP-T2NM-00000-00&context=1519360); [Telegraph Herald, 10/19/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:63WJ-B5Y1-JCDM-J006-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Although Hinson has said she supports expanding the child tax credit and some family care initiatives, she has consistently voted against Democrat-backed expansions, leading to claims of inconsistency and undermining support for working families ([Telegraph Herald, 9/21/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66FD-H8B1-DYWP-817X-00000-00&context=1519360); [Gazette, 11/12/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:642P-M7T1-DXVP-V157-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Hinson has called for stricter work requirements and targeted aid in programs like unemployment, food assistance, and child tax credits, a stance that could alienate lower-income or vulnerable constituents who may see these measures as barriers rather than support ([Gazette, 11/1/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69J2-NJV1-DXVP-T07P-00000-00&context=1519360); [Muscatine Journal, 7/25/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68SY-2T41-JBCN-34K1-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Her emphasis on rooting out fraud and restricting benefits, particularly regarding unemployment and Medicaid, can feed a perception that she prioritizes budget savings over robust assistance for those struggling the most ([Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier, 12/20/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64BV-P6V1-DXVP-V187-00000-00&context=1519360); [Gazette, 4/8/25](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FHT-CT53-RV0N-G16Y-00000-00&context=1519360)).
