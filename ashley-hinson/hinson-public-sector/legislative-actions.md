# Legislative Work and Congressional Actions

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson served as a member of the powerful House Appropriations and Budget Committees from her first term in Congress, giving her an influential role in federal funding decisions and a platform to advocate for Iowa's interests ([AshleHinson.com](https://ashleyhinson.com/meet-ashley/), [Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:61S3-V4N1-JBCN-40VW-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She prioritized targeted infrastructure and community investments, securing millions in federal earmark funding for projects across Iowa, particularly benefitting rural communities ([Muscatine Journal](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C77-27C1-JBCN-3122-00000-00&context=1519360), [Quad-City Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C6J-9P81-DXVP-T3R6-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson emphasized bipartisan work, co-sponsoring and helping pass several bills with Democrats, notably the Sgt. Ketchum Rural Veterans Mental Health Act and the Maternal and Child Health Stillbirth Prevention Act ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D7B-CCW1-DXVP-T2DK-00000-00&context=1519360), [Globe Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D7K-5HV1-JBCN-31HX-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She opposed large federal spending packages like the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and American Rescue Plan, yet actively sought and promoted funding for Iowa from those laws once passed (a position that drew criticism for perceived inconsistency) ([Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/iowa-gop-rep-ashley-hinson-slammed-touting-infrastructure-bill-she-voted-against-1671409), [Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66ST-7GN1-DYWP-8062-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson consistently advocated for fiscal discipline, opposing what she described as “wasteful” government spending and promoting targeted, local investments, particularly in appropriations and budget debates ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6476-FT01-DXVP-V3RD-00000-00&context=1519360), [Quad-City Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C6J-9P81-DXVP-T3R6-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Despite bipartisan efforts and legislative achievements, Hinson faced criticism for voting against significant infrastructure measures while later taking credit for their impact in her district, exposing a potential vulnerability in narratives about her legislative record ([Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:670N-4821-JBCN-430B-00000-00&context=1519360), [Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64KW-R2T1-JBCN-418V-00000-00&context=1519360)).
