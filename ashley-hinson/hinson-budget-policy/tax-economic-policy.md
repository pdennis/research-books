# Taxation and Economic Policy

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson has consistently supported making the 2017 Trump-era tax cuts permanent, arguing their expiration would result in substantial tax increases for Iowa families—citing figures such as a 25% hike or $1,300 more annually for the average family ([Des Moines Register](https://www.desmoinesregister.com/story/news/politics/2025/04/24/iowa-rep-ashley-hinson-town-hall-defends-donald-trump-agenda-mason-city/83229133007/), [Globe Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FGR-2693-SG72-X4T1-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson opposes tax increases on corporations and the wealthy, criticizing proposals by President Biden to raise corporate tax rates or implement new revenue measures ([Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62RB-RG11-DYWP-832K-00000-00&context=1519360), [OnTheIssues.org](https://ontheissues.org/House/Ashley_Hinson_Tax_Reform.htm#20PVS-11A)).
> * She has prioritized expanding the child tax credit for working families—proposing increased benefits, work requirements, and retroactive eligibility for unborn children—while opposing the 2021 American Rescue Plan Act's broader expansion without work requirements ([Sioux City Journal](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68SY-2T41-JBCN-33BP-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson consistently frames government spending and tax increases as drivers of inflation and economic hardship, frequently criticizing "Bidenomics" for rising costs and expressing concern over the tax impact on middle-class families ([Des Moines Register](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C10-S181-JB1V-G030-00000-00&context=1519360), [Daily Nonpareil](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62SR-CMY1-JBCN-4416-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She has voiced strong opposition to Biden’s student loan forgiveness plans, warning they would shift costs to taxpayers and disproportionately benefit higher-income Americans while fueling inflation ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:667X-F921-JBCN-42R5-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Potential vulnerability: Multiple letters to the editor and critics have accused Hinson of prioritizing tax breaks for the wealthy and corporations, and of supporting policies that could add to the national debt ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CXR-3481-JBCN-353V-00000-00&context=1519360), [Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69FH-1DW1-JBCN-335M-00000-00&context=1519360)).
