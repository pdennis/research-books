# Personal Religious Beliefs and Background

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson has publicly expressed the belief that rights come from God rather than the government, referencing her faith as a guiding principle in her public statements ([Des Moines Register, 11/7/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DC5-P5C1-DXGX-61F4-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * In April 2025, Hinson cited the attempted assassination of Donald Trump as evidence of divine intervention, stating her belief that God "saved the president" for a specific purpose ([Des Moines Register, 4/24/25](https://www.desmoinesregister.com/story/news/politics/2025/04/24/iowa-rep-ashley-hinson-town-hall-defends-donald-trump-agenda-mason-city/83229133007/); [Des Moines Register, 4/25/25](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FN6-KM03-RS8W-J18D-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson framed Trump’s survival as a sign that he is meant to help "save the country," underlining the connection she draws between faith, political events, and leadership ([Des Moines Register, 4/24/25](https://www.desmoinesregister.com/story/news/politics/2025/04/24/iowa-rep-ashley-hinson-town-hall-defends-donald-trump-agenda-mason-city/83229133007/)).
> * In a 2021 public prayer, Hinson was mentioned as an example of "the desire to serve," suggesting her service-mindedness is recognized among Iowa’s political and religious communities ([Daily Nonpareil, 4/2/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62C1-0H11-DXVP-V0FN-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson’s public reliance on divine purpose in political matters could present vulnerabilities, as critics may question the appropriation of religious belief in governance decisions.
