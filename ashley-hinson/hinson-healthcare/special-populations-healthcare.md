# Healthcare Access for Special Populations

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson advocated for increased investment in rural health facilities to better meet community health needs, emphasizing the importance of local access to care ([Telegraph Herald, 8/14/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CR6-9MT1-DYWP-81XP-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She highlighted the need for equitable healthcare access regardless of geographic location, stating that all people deserve healthcare providers no matter their zip code ([Telegraph Herald, 8/14/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CR6-9MT1-DYWP-81XP-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson identified persistent staffing shortages in rural Iowa hospitals, particularly difficulties recruiting specialists, which undermines health services in underserved areas ([Globe Gazette, 9/7/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6943-TN31-DXVP-T522-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She secured over $1 million in federal funding to expand and modernize rural maternity units and sought further investments to boost delivery capacities in underserved regions ([Daily Nonpareil, 5/15/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C29-R3X1-JBCN-3526-00000-00&context=1519360), [The Gazette, 5/17/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C28-X081-JBCN-30HN-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Despite these investments, challenges remain as some rural maternity facilities are still unable to meet regional demands, highlighting ongoing vulnerabilities in healthcare access for special populations ([Daily Nonpareil, 5/15/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C29-R3X1-JBCN-3526-00000-00&context=1519360)).
