# Legislative Actions Impacting Health Policy

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson signed on as a co-sponsor to bipartisan metastatic breast cancer legislation in November 2023, supporting earlier access to federal support and care for patients ([Waterloo Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CR3-0JF1-DY8S-B4V4-00000-00&context=1519360), [Daily Nonpareil](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CRN-RY31-DXVP-T016-00000-00&context=1519360), [Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D7B-CCW1-DXVP-T2DB-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson initially declined to co-sponsor the Metastatic Breast Cancer Access to Care Act in 2023, later announcing support and joining the bill as a co-sponsor after public attention ([Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69F3-36S1-JBCN-341M-00000-00&context=1519360), [Des Moines Register](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69F2-S5T1-JB1V-G2H1-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * In 2021, Hinson voted against HR 1195, a bill aimed at protecting health care workers from violence, citing disagreement with some provisions and concerns about the legislative process ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62X3-N101-DXVP-V4G2-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Sarah Corkery, a political opponent, criticized Hinson's record on health-related votes, pointing to issues such as mental health, gun safety, and drug pricing ([Des Moines Register](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69F2-S5T1-JB1V-G2H1-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Potential vulnerabilities include Hinson's initial reluctance to support key health legislation and opposition to health care worker protections, which have been targeted by critics.
