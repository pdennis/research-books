# COVID-19 and Public Health Emergencies

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson contracted COVID-19 twice (post-election 2020 and January 2022), experiencing mild symptoms both times, and publicly disclosed her status; she continued working remotely and followed CDC guidance during isolation ([Gazette](https://www.thegazette.com/government-politics/ashley-hinson-says-communication-skills-will-come-in-handy-in-washington/), [Waterloo Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64J5-01P1-JBTP-M3MX-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson publicly supported COVID-19 vaccination, received both the vaccine and booster, and encouraged Iowans to get vaccinated, but consistently opposed federal vaccine and mask mandates, favoring personal choice and responsibility ([Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:63MR-8881-DYWP-8185-00000-00&context=1519360), [Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64J5-81R1-JBCN-404Y-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She voted against major federal COVID-19 relief legislation including the American Rescue Plan Act, citing concerns that only a small portion was directly related to public health and virus response, and instead advocated for targeted relief, direct assistance, and expanded testing ([Des Moines Register](https://www.desmoinesregister.com/story/news/politics/2021/03/10/joe-biden-covid-19-relief-bill-iowa-cindy-axne-randy-feenstra-ashley-hinson-mariannette-miller-meeks/6947005002/), [Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64GF-WRV1-DXVP-V3RS-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson advocated for keeping schools and businesses open during the pandemic and opposed closures and mask mandates, emphasizing the need to avoid further disruptions and support children’s in-person education ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:65YT-JH51-JBCN-4435-00000-00&context=1519360), [Mitchell County Press-News](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64GF-WRV1-DXVP-V3RS-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She supported federal efforts to expand vaccine and testing access (especially for underserved and rural communities), highlighted the role of community health centers, and promoted telehealth, but at times was slow to get vaccinated, waiting until vaccine eligibility opened to all Iowans ([Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62FH-0N41-DXVP-V492-00000-00&context=1519360), [Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6207-T101-JCDM-J538-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Potential vulnerabilities include repeated COVID-19 infections despite vaccination and boosting, and criticism for voting against large-scale pandemic relief and vaccine funding ([Waterloo Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:623P-W6C1-JBTP-H3J8-00000-00&context=1519360)).
