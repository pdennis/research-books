# Reproductive and Maternal Health Policy

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson supported and led enactment of bipartisan stillbirth prevention legislation, enabling federal funds for education and research to reduce stillbirths and improve maternal health outcomes, especially in rural areas ([Gazette](https://www.thegazette.com/subject/news/health/stillbirth-prevention-bill-hinson-merkley-congress-passes-20240517), [Waterloo Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CGN-VY31-DY8S-B50G-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson consistently advocated for expanding access to maternity care, including requesting federal funding to increase rural hospital capacity, supporting legislation to expand midwifery services, and introducing a bill to improve newborn insurance coverage ([Waterloo-Cedar Falls Courier](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C79-S251-DXVP-T40M-00000-00&context=1519360), [Sioux City Journal](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68D8-HFT1-JBCN-32P5-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * She strongly supported access to IVF (in vitro fertilization), describing it as compatible with her pro-life views, and led efforts to expand IVF insurance coverage for federal employees ([Des Moines Register](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D7B-2B91-JB1V-G17C-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * On drug pricing and insulin, Hinson opposed government price controls and voted against Democratic-led $35 insulin cap bills, arguing these would raise premiums, while backing an alternative GOP bill capping insulin costs at $50/month for Medicare part D seniors ([Telegraph Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6555-FWG1-DYWP-80S7-00000-00&context=1519360), [Daily Nonpareil](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CRN-RY31-DXVP-T016-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Hinson supported expanded access to contraception, including over-the-counter birth control for adults and rural access, but voted against certain Democratic contraception protection bills citing concerns over provider rights and drug approval ([The Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:660F-FYF1-DXVP-V2W0-00000-00&context=1519360), [Sioux City Journal](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:660H-3PJ1-DXVP-V1RW-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * A potential vulnerability: Hinson initially declined to co-sponsor bipartisan legislation to support metastatic breast cancer patients, joining as co-sponsor only after public criticism ([Globe Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CRV-JYN1-JBCN-33F7-00000-00&context=1519360), [The Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69DW-3Y61-DXVP-T0B2-00000-00&context=1519360)).
