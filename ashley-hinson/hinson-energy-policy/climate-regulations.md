# Climate Change and Environmental Regulations

### Summary

> * Ashley Hinson consistently opposes broad federal environmental regulations, supporting the return to Trump-era “Waters of the U.S.” rules that limit federal oversight of wetlands and waterways, arguing that local control is preferable ([Gazette, Aug 2021](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:63G8-R471-DXVP-V34R-00000-00&context=1519360); [Todd Dorman - Gazette, Oct 2022](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66K9-5D31-DXVP-V07M-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * She prioritizes conservation and land management practices—including precision agriculture and targeted flood mitigation—over federal climate legislation, carbon taxes, or sweeping regulatory approaches ([Daily Nonpareil, Aug 2023](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:690Y-JN71-DXVP-T07P-00000-00&context=1519360); [Telegraph Herald, Aug 2022](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6647-SJM1-DYWP-81N1-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Hinson supports investment in carbon capture technology and favors tax credits to drive innovation in the agricultural sector, while emphasizing the need to protect landowner property rights and expressing caution about the use of eminent domain for pipeline projects ([Globe Gazette, Aug 2023](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6914-73J1-DXVP-T32G-00000-00&context=1519360); [New Hampton Tribune, Aug 2023](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68VV-TDX1-JBCN-342T-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * She does not support higher sales taxes or major new tax initiatives to fund water quality improvements but has endorsed state investment from existing revenue sources and backs models like the Middle Cedar Partnership Project ([Gazette, Jan 2017](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5MN6-9PT1-JD6S-R43R-00000-00&context=1519360); [Gazette, Oct 2016](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5MRJ-0MM1-JD6S-R23T-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Hinson has spread misleading information regarding the “Green New Deal,” conflating unrelated legislation and overstating its financial impact on Iowans and farmers; these claims received poor ratings from fact-checkers ([The Gazette, Apr 2022](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:656P-F381-DXVP-V0PM-00000-00&context=1519360); [Gazette, Sep 2020](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:60W0-35J1-JBCN-4081-00000-00&context=1519360)).
