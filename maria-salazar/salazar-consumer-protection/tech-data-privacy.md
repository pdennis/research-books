# Stance on Technology, Data, and Privacy

### Summary

-   María Elvira Salazar has taken a leading role in introducing the 'Take it Down' bill, which targets the issue of revenge pornography and unauthorized deepfake content ([El Nuevo Herald, 7/11/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CFV-4XX1-DYJM-M383-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She describes the rise of deepfake imagery as a major threat, warning that it can destroy the lives of innocent people and families.

-   Salazar refers to non-consensual deepfake images as a "cancer" that must be urgently addressed through strong legislation like the 'Take it Down' Act.

-   The bill aims to hold online platforms accountable for the presence and spread of non-consensual intimate images (NCII) and protect victims.

-   Salazar notes a key vulnerability for victims: it is often unclear who is responsible for posting NCII, complicating both enforcement and victim protection efforts.

### Privacy and Personal Data Protection

**July 2024: María Elvira Salazar Lideró La Presentación Del Proyecto De Ley** '**Take it Down**' **Contra Pornografía De Venganza** According to El Nuevo Herald, "El alarmante aumento de los deepfakes amenaza con destruir las vidas de personas y familias inocentes", dijo la representante María Elvira Salazar (R-FL), quien lidera el proyecto que presentó con Madeleine Dean (D-PA), August Pfluger (R-TX), Stacey Plaskett (D-VI), Vern Buchanan (R-FL) y Debbie Dingell (D-MI). \[El Nuevo Herald (Miami, Florida), [7/11/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CFV-4XX1-DYJM-M383-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

**July 2024: Salazar Declaró Que Los Deepfakes** '**Son Un Cáncer**' **Que Debe Ser Tratado Urgentemente** According to El Nuevo Herald, Salazar afirmó que "las imágenes deepfake no consensuadas son un cáncer que ya no puede dejar de tratarse. La Ley Take it Down es la mejor manera de responsabilizar a las plataformas en línea y proteger a las víctimas de estos horrendos crímenes". \[El Nuevo Herald (Miami, Florida), [7/11/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CFV-4XX1-DYJM-M383-00000-00&context=1519360)\]
