# Family Background and Early Life

### Summary

-   Maria Elvira Salazar was born in Miami's Little Havana neighborhood to parents who were Cuban exiles, making her part of the Cuban-American community in South Florida ([Floricua News](https://floricuanews.com/2024/05/13/10-things-to-know-about-congresswoman-maria-elvira-salazar/), [Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5TF4-6XX1-DYJM-M49M-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Salazar frequently cites her Cuban exile background and upbringing to connect with voters, emphasizing her family's experiences escaping communism and her understanding of the immigrant journey ([El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67G6-8JY1-JC3J-X0V2-00000-00&context=1519360), [Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5TF4-6XX1-DYJM-M49M-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She spent part of her childhood in Puerto Rico, which broadened her perspective on Latin American communities in the U.S. ([Floricua News](https://floricuanews.com/2024/05/13/10-things-to-know-about-congresswoman-maria-elvira-salazar/)).

-   Salazar describes herself as a "daughter of Miami" and a product of the city, highlighting her background as both a local and a former journalist ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5TF4-6XX1-DYJM-M49M-00000-00&context=1519360), [El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5TDG-XCY1-JC3J-X0RN-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Her personal history and her parents' escape from Cuba have shaped her anti-socialism stance and her political messaging ([El American](https://elamerican.com/maria-elvira-salazar-me-preocupa-petro-presidente/?lang=es)).

-   There is one vulnerability in the public record: a 2021 article from the Florida Times-Union incorrectly reported that she was born in Peru and immigrated at age 12, which is at odds with consistent local reporting identifying her as a Miami-born daughter of Cuban exiles ([Florida Times-Union](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:635D-47R1-JBSS-G3YN-00000-00&context=1519360)).
