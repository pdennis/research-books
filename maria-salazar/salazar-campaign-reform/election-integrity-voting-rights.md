# Election Integrity and Voting Rights

### Summary

-   María Elvira Salazar cast several public votes supporting the certification of the 2020 presidential election results, including Pennsylvania's electoral votes for Joe Biden ([Bradenton Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66T9-GN31-DYJM-M1X0-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Salazar faced criticism and scrutiny for amplifying false or debunked claims about election fraud, particularly regarding results in Pennsylvania, including repeating conspiracy theories on Spanish-language radio ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66N1-XCD1-DYJM-M0P7-00000-00&context=1519360); [El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:61T4-1DJ1-JC3J-X37W-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She voted against major voting rights legislation aimed at expanding access to the ballot, including HR 1, which sought to broaden voter participation and overhaul campaign finance rules ([Sarasota Herald Tribune](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6256-NHR1-DXVP-V244-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Salazar broke ranks with most House Republicans to support the creation of a bipartisan commission to investigate the January 6 attack on the Capitol, citing strong support from Capitol police ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62PY-0DW1-DYJM-M4W1-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She has been criticized by editorial boards for failing to condemn election misinformation strongly enough, which some consider a vulnerability relating to her stance on election integrity ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66N1-XCD1-DYJM-M0P7-00000-00&context=1519360); [El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:61T4-1DJ1-JC3J-X37W-00000-00&context=1519360)).

### Certification of Elections and Electoral College

#### Views on Election Legitimacy

**2021: Fabiola Santiago Alleged María Elvira Salazar Supported Claims of Election Fraud** According to an opinion piece by Fabiola Santiago in El Nuevo Herald, "En lugar de liderar el camino para salir de esta época de pesadilla en la historia de Estados Unidos como otros republicanos lo están tratando de hacer, \[...\] María Elvira Salazar \[...\] dieron crédito a las falsas teorías de conspiración con sus votos." \[Fabiola Santiago - El Nuevo Herald, [1/18/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:61T4-1DJ1-JC3J-X37W-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

**2021: Salazar Suggested Possible Election Fraud In Pennsylvania** According to El Nuevo Herald, "Meses después de ganar su elección, la legisladora sugirió en la radio en español que podría haber habido fraude en las elecciones de Pennsylvania, una afirmación falsa que ha sido difundida por el ex presidente Donald Trump." \[El Nuevo Herald (Miami, Florida), [10/24/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66PB-CSN1-JC3J-X4JH-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

#### Statements on Election Fraud Claims

**2021: Fabiola Santiago Claimed María Elvira Salazar Failed to Condemn Election Misinformation** According to an opinion piece by Fabiola Santiago in El Nuevo Herald, "En lugar de poner fin a las extravagantes mentiras sobre el fraude electoral difundidas por sus electores y difundidas ampliamente como si fueran verdades absolutas, se prestaron a los intereses estratégicos del Partido Republicano de Trump, atendiendo la ira en sectores de Miami-Dade que se niegan a aceptar la elección presidencial del demócrata Joe Biden." \[Fabiola Santiago - El Nuevo Herald, [1/18/21](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:61T4-1DJ1-JC3J-X37W-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

#### Statements on Election Fraud Claims

**2021: María Salazar Repeated Debunked Claims About Pennsylvania Votes After 2020 Election** According to an editorial published in Miami Herald, "during an appearance on Spanish-language radio, she repeated a false narrative about the integrity of the vote in Pennsylvania. 'How is it possible that in Pennsylvania, there are 200,000 more votes on Election Day than there were \[voters\] in the electoral rolls? That's not possible,' she told Radio Mambi on Jan. 11, 2021. She appeared to be citing a repeatedly debunked conspiracy theory based on incomplete data." \[Editorial - Miami Herald, [10/18/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66N1-XCD1-DYJM-M0P7-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

**2022: Miami Herald Editorial Board Criticized María Salazar for Spreading Election Misinformation** According to an editorial published in Miami Herald, "However, it is Salazar's willingness to spread misinformation about the 2020 election that defined her worst moment. The truth should prevail over partisanship." \[Editorial - Miami Herald, [10/18/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66N1-XCD1-DYJM-M0P7-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

**2021: María Elvira Salazar Repeated Trump's False Claims About Pennsylvania Election Results** According to an opinion piece by Andres Oppenheimer in El Nuevo Herald, "Salazar no votó ese día porque estaba con COVID-19, pero poco después repitió las mentiras de Trump sobre los resultados del estado de Pennsylvania en una entrevista con Radio Mambi." \[Andres Oppenheimer - El Nuevo Herald, [10/21/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66NT-TXS1-JC3J-X367-00000-00&context=1519360)\]
