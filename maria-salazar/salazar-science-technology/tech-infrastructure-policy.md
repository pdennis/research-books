# Technology and Infrastructure Policy

### Summary

-   Rep. María Elvira Salazar has been a leading advocate for expanding internet access in Cuba, repeatedly urging the Biden Administration to approve technological solutions---such as aerial wireless networks, satellite internet, and enhanced embassy-area signals---to support Cuban protestors and counter government censorship ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6353-9X81-JC3J-X125-00000-00&context=1519360), [El Nuevo Herald](https://elamerican.com/maria-elvira-salazar-me-preocupa-petro-presidente/?lang=es)).

-   Salazar emphasized that permission from the federal government, not funding, is the main obstacle to deploying internet solutions for Cuba, noting that the Cuban-American community is willing to provide necessary financial resources if authorized ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6353-9X81-JC3J-X125-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She called on figures like Elon Musk to supply satellite technology to Cuba, modeled after the Starlink deployment in Ukraine, and introduced the Operation Starfall bill to improve communication access during government internet shutdowns ([El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64XV-NC31-JC3J-X239-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   In 2024, Salazar led a bipartisan effort against AI-driven exploitation of people's images and voices, introducing the No AI FRAUD Act to establish federal protections and impose hefty fines for unauthorized digital cloning ([Miami New Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BBC-TXF1-DXVP-T1KH-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Salazar has also pressed for easing federal restrictions on the cruise industry, criticizing CDC policies she viewed as excessively restrictive and advocating for the industry's safe reopening post-pandemic ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62DC-GS91-JC3J-X1V4-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   A potential vulnerability in Salazar's internet access proposals is their reliance on federal approval; without executive backing, even community-funded solutions face legal and logistical barriers.
