# Stance on Border Security and Enforcement

### Summary

-   Salazar consistently supports strong border security, advocating for increased spending on border technology and personnel, and previously backing Trump's border priorities---though she stopped short of explicitly endorsing a border wall ([Miami Herald, 10/23/18](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5TJN-PNC1-DYJM-M4TR-00000-00&context=1519360); [El Nuevo Herald, 10/22/18](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5TJB-6B71-JC3J-X3XC-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She opposed Republican proposals to severely restrict asylum at the U.S.-Mexico border, warning such measures undermine traditional U.S. protections for people fleeing persecution---a stance praised by multiple editorial boards ([Miami Herald, 2/1/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67FN-0B91-JC3J-X0RV-00000-00&context=1519360); [El Nuevo Herald, 2/3/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67G6-8JY1-JC3J-X0V2-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Salazar calls for robust enforcement against criminals and employers who violate immigration laws, proposing national E-Verify mandates for employers and expressing support for deporting gang members, but insists on protecting law-abiding undocumented immigrants and asylum seekers from deportation ([Miami Herald, 2/8/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64R9-DK71-JC3J-X415-00000-00&context=1519360); [Miami Herald, 1/31/25](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F1F-2583-RS40-G0VM-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She actively defends special protections and due process for Cuban, Venezuelan, Nicaraguan, and Haitian migrants---especially those arriving under parole or TPS programs---and frequently intervenes with federal agencies to prevent their deportation ([Miami Herald, 1/24/25](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DYV-JG73-RRWH-D38J-00000-00&context=1519360); [Islander News, 1/30/25](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F1J-B6N3-RS65-G00D-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Salazar has publicly criticized both Biden and Trump immigration policies at times---blaming Biden for border chaos and child trafficking, and expressing concerns about Trump's mass deportation rhetoric and its potential impacts in Miami-Dade, especially for non-criminal migrants and families ([Miami Herald, 10/24/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66P9-36F1-JC3J-X32S-00000-00&context=1519360); [Tampa Bay Times, 7/17/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CH3-9K61-JCBJ-Y00J-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Potential vulnerability: Salazar has occasionally made controversial remarks--including an inflammatory comment about violent gang members and earlier positions supporting deportation for some long-term undocumented residents---that have drawn criticism and required clarification ([Miami Herald, 10/11/24](https://www.miamiherald.com/news/politics-government/article293713594.html); [Miami Herald, 4/15/25](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FK3-CGK3-S619-H0JK-00000-00&context=1519360)).
