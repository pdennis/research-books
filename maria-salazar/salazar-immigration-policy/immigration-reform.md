# Comprehensive Immigration Reform Legislation and Proposals

### Summary

-   Salazar's Dignity Act has faced skepticism from both parties---Democrats criticize it for being too strict, while some Republicans label it as "amnesty," leading to limited legislative progress ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69P9-S3R1-DY37-F05X-00000-00&context=1519360), [South Florida Sun-Sentinel](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69P9-S3R1-DY37-F05X-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She has avoided direct public confrontation with the Trump administration on controversial immigration issues, leading to criticism from Democratic opponents who accuse her of remaining silent when allies push harsh enforcement policies ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FK9-BP43-RWJ4-R1WX-00000-00&context=1519360), [El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FK9-BP43-RWJ4-R1X7-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Although Salazar frames her immigration proposals as bipartisan and humane, advocacy groups and some Hispanic leaders attack her for not doing enough to protect immigrants facing deportation, putting her at odds with parts of her district ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FGS-BTF3-RW8D-118G-00000-00&context=1519360), [El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FH0-B0C3-RSCB-V22G-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   At times, Salazar has shifted blame for immigrant hardships onto Democratic administrations, which exposes her to charges of deflecting rather than confronting root causes or Republican policy failures ([Editorial - Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FKJ-08N3-RRS3-50GN-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   The financial requirements and long legalization timelines in her proposals (such as \$10,000 in payments and up to 15 years to citizenship) risk alienating both immigrant advocates and GOP hardliners, potentially undermining grassroots support and bipartisan appeal ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6899-K731-JC3J-X43F-00000-00&context=1519360), [Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64X4-SVD1-DYTM-N3C8-00000-00&context=1519360)).
