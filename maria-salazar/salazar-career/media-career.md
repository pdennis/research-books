# Television and Media Career

### Summary

-   María Elvira Salazar had a prominent career as a Spanish-language television journalist and anchor spanning over 30 years, working for major networks like Telemundo, Univision, CNN en Español, Mega TV, and América TeVé ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5TF4-6XX1-DYJM-M49M-00000-00&context=1519360), [El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D5F-FW61-DYJM-M04X-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She built her reputation covering major events in Latin America, serving as Univision's Central America bureau chief, reporting from El Salvador and Nicaragua, and conducting high-profile interviews, including with Fidel Castro (1995), Augusto Pinochet, and Nicolás Maduro ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5TF4-6XX1-DYJM-M49M-00000-00&context=1519360), [South Florida Sun-Sentinel](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5T4T-9FM1-DY37-F261-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Salazar hosted several influential programs, such as "María Elvira," "María Elvira Live," and "De Noche Con María Elvira," earning strong recognition among Hispanic audiences in Miami and helping shape political debate on Cuban and Latin American issues ([El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5HTH-B6N1-DYJM-M0F0-00000-00&context=1519360), [Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5TNP-46V1-DYJM-M210-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Her interview with Fidel Castro became a subject of political scrutiny, with critics using segments to accuse her of being too friendly toward the dictator; Salazar defended her impartiality, described the interview as a tough journalistic achievement, and pointed to her longstanding criticism of the Cuban regime ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5SY9-CC51-JC3J-X38G-00000-00&context=1519360), [El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5SY8-SS01-DYJM-M4HW-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Salazar's media presence and decades-long TV career gave her strong name recognition, particularly among older Spanish-speaking and Cuban-American voters, significantly contributing to her later success in politics ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5TH1-X7N1-JC3J-X2J7-00000-00&context=1519360), [Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C23-3BM1-DYTM-N003-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She ended her journalism career in 2020--2021 after being elected to Congress, as required by House ethics rules ([El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6668-9PH1-DYJM-M14R-00000-00&context=1519360), [Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:63K3-15T1-JC3J-X46S-00000-00&context=1519360)).
