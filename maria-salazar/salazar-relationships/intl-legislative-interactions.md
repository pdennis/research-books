# International and Legislative Interactions

### Summary

-   María Elvira Salazar has publicly supported controversial international figures, including former Colombian President Iván Duque and Salvadoran President Nayib Bukele, attending Bukele's second inauguration and congratulating him on his reelection ([Miami Herald, 6/5/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C65-6611-DYJM-M032-00000-00), [El Nuevo Herald, 6/6/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C6F-VSK1-DYJM-M02N-00000-00)).

-   Salazar has used strong rhetoric against left-leaning leaders, calling Colombian President Gustavo Petro a "thief, a socialist, a Marxist, a terrorist" and expressing that "we need a Bukele in Cuba, Venezuela and Nicaragua" ([Miami Herald, 11/3/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66SF-F6V1-DYJM-M03W-00000-00), [Miami Herald, 6/5/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C65-6611-DYJM-M032-00000-00)).

-   Her alignment with Bukele has drawn criticism from editorial boards, highlighting potential inconsistencies with her hardline stance against dictatorships and raising concerns about supporting a strongman leader in a region increasingly influenced by Russia and China ([Miami Herald, 6/5/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C65-6611-DYJM-M032-00000-00), [El Nuevo Herald, 6/6/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C6F-VSK1-DYJM-M02N-00000-00)).

-   Salazar was targeted by Cuba's Communist Party media with personal and sexist attacks, which included unsubstantiated claims about her involvement with the CIA and terrorism ([Miami Herald, 2/2/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6B7T-N9G1-DYJM-M027-00000-00)).

-   She has publicly questioned the independence of Cuba's private sector, labeling it a government "ruse" during a congressional hearing and frequently using social media to criticize the Biden administration's approach to Cuban affairs ([Miami Herald, 2/2/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6B7T-N9G1-DYJM-M027-00000-00), [El Nuevo Herald, 12/11/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69VC-VY41-JC3J-X3C5-00000-00)).

-   Potential vulnerabilities include accusations of political inconsistency---namely, advocating against dictators while supporting controversial leaders---and negative attention drawn from harsh international rhetoric and media attacks.
