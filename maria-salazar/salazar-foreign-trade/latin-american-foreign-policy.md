# Positions on Latin American Foreign Policy

### Summary

-   María Elvira Salazar is a leading congressional voice against socialism and leftist regimes in Latin America, continuously condemning countries like Cuba, Venezuela, and Nicaragua for human rights abuses and authoritarianism, and linking socialism to oppression and violence ([El American](https://elamerican.com/maria-elvira-salazar-me-preocupa-petro-presidente/?lang=es), [Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:61XV-5561-DYJM-M29F-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She has introduced and supported legislation to maintain or increase U.S. sanctions on Cuba and Venezuela, such as the FORCE and VALOR Acts, and has called for continued designation of Cuba as a state sponsor of terrorism unless there are clear democratic reforms ([Free Press](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67BX-86D1-DXVP-V0H8-00000-00&context=1519360), [Islander News](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D0R-MDM1-DXVP-T0SX-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Salazar criticizes both the Biden administration and left-leaning Latin American governments, arguing U.S. policy has been too weak, has failed to support democratic opposition, and has allowed China and Russia to extend their influence in the region ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68DR-SMG1-JC3J-X1CH-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She has denounced efforts by authoritarian governments to create a false impression of economic reform (e.g., questioning the legitimacy of the Cuban private sector) and repeatedly states that only those connected to regime elites benefit ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6B66-HJG1-DYJM-M02K-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Salazar supports assertive U.S. engagement and aid strategies in Latin America, including vaccine diplomacy, democratic promotion, and cooperation on security and economic development, while rejecting negotiations or business with dictators ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62NV-G4T1-JC3J-X291-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Potential vulnerability: Her hardline positions and rhetoric, including broad use of terms like "socialist" and "terrorist" for a wide range of political opponents, have drawn criticism and accusations of partisanship or oversimplification of complex international issues ([NBC - 8 WFLA](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67S4-DCW1-DXVP-T2WP-00000-00&context=1519360)).
