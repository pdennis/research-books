# Family Background and Early Life

### Summary

-   Anna Paulina Luna was born Anna Paulina Mayerhofer in 1989 in Southern California to an unmarried Mexican-American mother and a father of Mexican and German descent ([Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2022/11/08/anna-paulina-luna-congress-instagram-maga/), [Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:611W-CYD1-DYTM-N0H1-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Luna's childhood was marked by instability, poverty, and family challenges, including frequent moves, living in low-income neighborhoods, and periods in the welfare system ([TIME](https://time.com/6292871/anna-paulina-luna-influencer-congress/), [Fox 13 News](https://www.fox13news.com/news/rising-gop-star-anna-paulina-luna-handily-wins-primary-faces-charlie-crist-in-november)).

-   Her father struggled with drug addiction and was in and out of jail throughout her upbringing, while her maternal grandmother died of HIV/AIDS from heroin use ([Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67KX-D231-DYTM-N14V-00000-00&context=1519360), [voteannapaulina.com](https://www.voteannapaulina.com/post/the-influencer-who-came-to-congress)).

-   Luna did not grow up speaking Spanish; her family emphasized English to shield children from racism ([Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:611W-CYD1-DYTM-N0H1-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She legally changed her last name to "Luna" in 2019 to honor her maternal grandmother's Mexican heritage, and her public image shifted over time, with opponents claiming she adapted her persona for different contexts ([Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2023/02/10/anna-paulina-luna-republican-biography/), [Floridian Press](https://floridianpress.com/2021/10/makki-accuses-former-obama-supporter-anna-paulina-luna-of-being-a-phony/)).

-   Luna has cited her challenging upbringing---marked by crime, loss, and frequent school changes---as motivation for her life and political career ([The Free Press (Tampa, Florida)](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67KV-9RB1-JBCN-4294-00000-00&context=1519360), [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2023/02/10/anna-paulina-luna-republican-biography/)).

### Family Heritage and Upbringing

#### Parental Background

There's a lot of involvement of me growing up in the welfare system," Luna said. \[Fox 13 News, [8/21/20](https://www.fox13news.com/news/rising-gop-star-anna-paulina-luna-handily-wins-primary-faces-charlie-crist-in-november)\]

**1989: Luna Was Born As Anna Paulina Mayerhofer In Santa Ana, California** According to Washington Post, 'Luna was born in 1989 in Santa Ana, Calif., as Anna Paulina Mayerhofer.' \[Washington Post, [11/8/22](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2022/11/08/anna-paulina-luna-congress-instagram-maga/)\]

**1989: Anna Paulina Luna Was Born In Orange City, California** According to Historica Wiki, "Anna Paulina Mayerhofer was born in Orange City, California in 1989, the daughter of a German and Mexican-American father and a Mexican-American mother; her paternal grandfather served in the Wehrmacht during World War II." \[Historica Wiki, [1/18/25](https://historica.fandom.com/wiki/Anna_Paulina_Luna)\]

**Luna Was Born Anna Paulina Mayerhofer In 1989 In Santa Ana, California** According to Tampa Bay Times, "Luna was born Anna Paulina Mayerhofer in 1989 in Santa Ana, Calif. Her mother is of Mexican descent while her father is Mexican and German." \[Tampa Bay Times, [10/6/20](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:611W-CYD1-DYTM-N0H1-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

**Luna Discussed Her Childhood And Her Father's Addiction** According to Tampa Bay Times, "Luna has talked in the past about her childhood and her relationship with her father, whom she said had a severe drug addiction when she was a child." \[Tampa Bay Times, [1/12/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64HP-6RX1-JCBJ-Y05F-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

**Luna Claimed Her Father Was In And Out Of Jail; Documentation Was Reviewed By Times Reporter** According to Tampa Bay Times, "Luna's office let a Times reporter review, but not copy, a document that the office said was produced by the California Department of Justice. It listed Mayerhofer's criminal charges and sentences through the years. The document showed he did several stints in local jails starting in 1990, when Luna was an infant." \[Tampa Bay Times, [2/14/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67KX-D231-DYTM-N14V-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

#### Cultural Influences

**2017: Luna Stated She Was Able To Take On Different Personalities For Modeling Work** According to Floridian Press, "In a 2017 interview with Canadian lifestyle and culture magazine Skyn, Luna --- who at the time went by her married name, Gamberzky --- described her biggest revelation in appearing before the camera: 'I'm able to take on different personalities depending on what image I am going for. I think getting into character of what you are selling is super important.'" \[Floridian Press, [10/1/21](https://floridianpress.com/2021/10/makki-accuses-former-obama-supporter-anna-paulina-luna-of-being-a-phony/)\]

**2019: Luna Legally Changed Her Name To Honor Maternal Grandmother's Hispanic Heritage** According to Washington Post, 'In 2019, according to the Tampa Bay Times, she filed paperwork with the Clark County Clerk in Washington state, where she and Gamberzky owned a home, to change her last name. She chose a name from her maternal grandmother, who came from Mexico City, to honor her Hispanic heritage, Luna's mother, Monica Todd, said at the Yacht Club. Todd said that her family was Mexican and that her daughter's father was half-German, half-Mexican.' \[Washington Post, [11/8/22](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2022/11/08/anna-paulina-luna-congress-instagram-maga/)\]

**2023: Luna's Public Persona Shifted Over Time** According to Washington Post, "Known then by her given last name of Mayerhofer, Luna sported designer clothing and expressed support for then-President Barack Obama. By the time she ran for Congress as a Republican, she had changed her last name to Luna in what she said was an homage to her mother's family." \[Washington Post, [2/10/23](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2023/02/10/anna-paulina-luna-republican-biography/)\]

**Luna Did Not Grow Up Speaking Spanish Despite Hispanic Heritage** According to Tampa Bay Times, "She didn't grow up speaking Spanish. Going back generations in her mother's family, children were taught English only, to shield them from pervasive racism." \[Tampa Bay Times, [10/6/20](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:611W-CYD1-DYTM-N0H1-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

**Luna Claimed Humble Beginnings And U.S. Air Force Service** According to Tampa Bay Times, "On the campaign trail, Luna often drew on her Hispanic heritage, her U.S. Air Force service and her humble beginnings in Southern California as the daughter of a man struggling with addiction and a woman navigating single motherhood." \[Tampa Bay Times, [2/14/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67KX-D231-DYTM-N14V-00000-00&context=1519360)\]
