# 2022 and 2024 Congressional Campaigns

### Summary

-   Anna Paulina Luna ran for Florida's 13th Congressional District in both 2022 and 2024, successfully winning the seat in 2022 after previously losing to Charlie Crist in 2020 and benefiting from redistricting that made the district more favorable to Republicans ([Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66TN-G371-JCBJ-Y519-00000-00&context=1519360), [MSNBC](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66TN-PY31-F053-D0S7-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Luna's 2022 campaign was supported by former President Donald Trump, as well as endorsements from several conservative figures and PACs; she emphasized themes like border security, energy independence, and fighting corruption ([Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66MY-YCT1-DYTM-N00N-00000-00&context=1519360), [Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:660K-T5G1-JC3J-X532-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Luna defeated Democrat Eric Lynn by approximately 8 points in 2022, becoming Florida's first Mexican-American Congresswoman from the district, and ran a grassroots campaign despite being significantly outspent ([Al Dia News](https://aldianews.com/en/politics/women-politics/who-anna-paulina-luna), [NBC - 8 WFLA](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6731-FD01-DXVP-V4FC-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   During her tenure, Luna aligned herself with far-right members of Congress and maintained Trump's endorsement; she expressed ongoing support for America First policies and opposition to progressive issues like COVID-19 vaccine mandates and gender theory in schools ([Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66MY-YCT1-DYTM-N00N-00000-00&context=1519360), [NBC - 8 WFLA](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:679C-0DJ1-DXVP-V39V-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   In her 2024 reelection bid, Luna faced multiple Democratic challengers, including Whitney Fox, and fundraising concerns surfaced as her opponents at times outpaced her; her district was deemed "potentially vulnerable" by the DCCC and Cook Political Report, noting that national Democrats targeted her seat ([Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68JX-JS31-JCBJ-Y0H2-00000-00&context=1519360), [NBC - 8 WFLA](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CYT-FCF1-JBCN-340P-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Late-campaign polls in 2024 showed a close contest between Luna and Fox, and Democrats criticized Luna's record on women's rights, climate, and Social Security, although Luna continued to campaign on conservative priorities ([Islander News](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D0R-MDM1-DXVP-T0T3-00000-00&context=1519360), [Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6B72-D7C1-JCBJ-Y002-00000-00&context=1519360)).
