# Family Background and Heritage

### Summary

-   Anna Paulina Luna was born Anna Paulina Mayerhofer in Santa Ana, California, in 1989 and previously used different surnames, including Gamberzky after marriage; she legally changed her last name to Luna in 2019 to represent her Hispanic heritage ([Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2023/02/10/anna-paulina-luna-republican-biography/), [Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:611W-CYD1-DYTM-N0H1-00000-00&context=1519360), [The Sun](https://www.the-sun.com/news/3102750/who-anna-paulina-luna/)).

-   Despite claiming Hispanic and Mexican roots, Luna identified as "White, not of Hispanic origin" on official documents such as her California driver's license and Florida voter registration in 2015 ([Central Florida Post](https://centralfloridapost.com/2021/12/14/anna-paulina-luna-gone-wild-lies-plague-campaign-of-former-stripper/), [Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/anna-paulina-luna-embellish-messianic-jewish-hispanic-heritage-2023-2)).

-   Luna's family background is ethnically mixed: her father was half-Mexican and half-German with a documented criminal record, while her maternal side included a grandmother who was a heroin addict and died from HIV-related complications ([TIME](https://time.com/6292871/anna-paulina-luna-influencer-congress/)).

-   Reports revealed Luna's paternal grandfather served in the German Wehrmacht during World War II, but her spokesperson emphasized her strong support for Israel and shared that her maternal great-grandfathers served with the U.S. military in WWII ([Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/anna-paulina-luna-slammed-over-report-she-lied-about-being-jewish-1780538)).

-   Luna is recognized as the first Mexican-American woman elected to Congress from Florida, but her personal accounts of family support during her upbringing have been disputed by relatives ([The Sun](https://www.the-sun.com/news/3102750/who-anna-paulina-luna/), [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2023/02/10/anna-paulina-luna-republican-biography/)).

-   Luna has publicly discussed criticism over her skin tone and perceptions of her racial identity, noting that some have told her she appears "too white" to identify as Hispanic ([Washington Examiner](https://www.washingtonexaminer.com/news/republican-anna-paulina-luna-lays-out-plan-to-flip-charlie-crists-florida-district), [Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64P1-KJ41-JCBJ-Y4CD-00000-00&context=1519360)).

### Ethnic and Cultural Heritage

#### Paternal heritage

**2023: Luna's Father Had Criminal Record And Drug Problems According To Family And Court Records** According to TIME, "On at least five different occasions, her half-Mexican, half-German father, George Mayerhofer, was sentenced to Orange County jail, according to records reviewed by TIME, on charges ranging from carrying a loaded firearm in public, probation violation, and driving on a suspended license. He also faced convictions for assault and battery and possession of a controlled substance." \[TIME, [7/10/23](https://time.com/6292871/anna-paulina-luna-influencer-congress/)\]

**Luna's Paternal Grandfather Served In German Wehrmacht In WWII** According to Newsweek, "Additionally, the report by the Post further revealed that Luna reportedly had Nazi roots, with several family members saying that Heinrich Mayerhofer, who died in 2003, served in the armed forces of Nazi Germany when he was a teenager in the 1940s." \[Newsweek, [2/10/23](https://www.newsweek.com/anna-paulina-luna-slammed-over-report-she-lied-about-being-jewish-1780538)\]

**Simon Wiesenthal Center Experts Said Luna's Grandfather's Uniform Was Wehrmacht** According to Newsweek, "The photo was reviewed by experts at the Simon Wiesenthal Center who confirmed that Heinrich's uniform matched that of a member of the Wehrmacht, the military of Nazi Germany." \[Newsweek, [2/10/23](https://www.newsweek.com/anna-paulina-luna-slammed-over-report-she-lied-about-being-jewish-1780538)\]

**2022: Luna Stated Receiving Criticism Based On Her Skin Tone** According to Tampa Bay Times, "She said as a woman, she feels she gets extra criticism from people attempting to police her speech based on her skin tone, including people saying she's too white to identify as Hispanic." \[Tampa Bay Times, [2/1/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:64P1-KJ41-JCBJ-Y4CD-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

#### Maternal heritage
