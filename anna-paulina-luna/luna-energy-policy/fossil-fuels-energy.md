# Fossil Fuels and Domestic Energy Production

### Summary

-   Anna Paulina Luna has consistently supported increasing domestic energy production, backing slogans like "American Oil from American Soil" and advocating for a ban on U.S. oil exports to boost domestic supply ([FOX 13](https://www.fox13news.com/news/one-on-one-with-anna-paulina-luna-her-views-on-banning-oil-exports-pork-infrastructure-spending-vaccines), [Tampa Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CD1-RXH1-JCBJ-Y0MF-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She has criticized Biden administration policies on electric vehicles (EVs), calling EV mandates unrealistic, unaffordable for most Americans, and difficult for the current U.S. electrical grid to support ([Fox Business Network](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BRF-YM81-F15W-H0M2-00000-00&context=1519360), [Fox News](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:689P-BDV1-JCS1-648T-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Luna has argued that traditional gasoline and diesel vehicles remain crucial, especially for emergency and disaster response situations ([Fox Business Network](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BRF-YM81-F15W-H0M2-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She has raised concerns over foreign influence and supply chains, pointing out China's dominance in lithium production for EV batteries and expressing alarm over iGas USA's ties to China after co-signing letters supporting the company's stance with the EPA ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69N1-XYP1-JC3J-X3W4-00000-00&context=1519360), [Orlando Sentinel](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69N7-X4K1-JBM5-R3NT-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Potential vulnerability: Luna co-signed letters supporting iGas USA and later stated she was "disturbed" by the company's Chinese ties, inviting scrutiny over initial support followed by concerns after-the-fact ([Miami Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69N1-XYP1-JC3J-X3W4-00000-00&context=1519360), [El Nuevo Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:69N3-7MV1-JC3J-X029-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   She also linked policies in states like California to population decline, arguing that restrictive environmental policies are prompting residents to move to states with more favorable energy policies ([Fox Business Network](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BRF-YM81-F15W-H0M2-00000-00&context=1519360)).

### Support for Oil and Gas Industries

#### Views on expanded drilling

**2022: Anna Paulina Luna Supported A Ban On U.S. Oil Exports** In an interview with FOX 13, Anna Paulina Luna said, "The United States has literally one of the biggest supplies of cleanest oil in the entire world. There's no reason why we need to be going to places like Saudi Arabia or even Venezuela to get those oil source." When asked if she would support a ban in order to increase the domestic supply, Luna responded, "Frankly, yes." \[Interview - Anna Paulina Luna with FOX 13, [8/12/22](https://www.fox13news.com/news/one-on-one-with-anna-paulina-luna-her-views-on-banning-oil-exports-pork-infrastructure-spending-vaccines)\]

**2024: Luna Claimed Minority Voters Are Pulling Away From Biden Due To EV Policies** In an interview with Fox Business Network, Rep. Anna Paulina Luna said, "ironically enough, you know, we're seeing that a lot of minorities, specifically Hispanic Americans are pulling away from their support of Joe Biden also correlate to the fact that I believe that they're not supporting EVs as well." \[Interview - Rep. Anna Paulina Luna with Fox Business Network, [4/5/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BRF-YM81-F15W-H0M2-00000-00&context=1519360)\]
