# Policy Positions and Platform

### Summary

-   Opposition to federal infrastructure funding: Begich condemned Don Young for supporting the 2021 Bipartisan Infrastructure Bill, raising concerns he would deprive Alaska of needed resources for broadband, sewers, roads, and ports ([Fairbanks Daily News-Miner](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:65BP-7K91-DXVP-V1GG-00000-00&context=1519360), [Juneau Empire](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DCB-4651-F0HF-82GG-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Strong ties to partisan and national GOP agendas: Begich's rhetoric aligns closely with national Republican and "America First" priorities, which could alienate moderates and those wary of partisan conflict ([Anchorage Daily News](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DBY-D4T1-DYRB-S4K6-00000-00&context=1519360), [Kodiak Daily Mirror](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DC0-W4R1-JBCN-31C7-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Prioritizing resource development over environmental concerns: Begich's platform emphasizes maximizing oil, fishing, and mineral extraction while downplaying or dismissing environmental regulations, which could draw criticism from environmentalists and some local communities ([Anchorage Daily News](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BVR-1481-JC89-2019-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Support for polarizing social policies: He has publicly supported book bans and restrictions on transgender athletes, which may alienate socially liberal and moderate constituents ([Anchorage Daily News](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BVR-1481-JC89-2019-00000-00&context=1519360), [Bristol Bay Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BW5-KJF1-JBCN-306S-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Opposition to ranked choice voting: Begich has advocated repealing Alaska's ranked choice voting system, risking backlash from voters who favor the new system and believe it gives them more choice ([Anchorage Daily News](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CF4-S871-JC89-2003-00000-00&context=1519360), [Juneau Empire](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CVD-0Y31-F0HF-804X-00000-00&context=1519360)).
