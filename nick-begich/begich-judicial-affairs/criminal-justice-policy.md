# Criminal Justice and Law Enforcement Policy

### Summary

-   Nick Begich emphasized the importance of the rule of law and stated that those responsible for the January 6, 2021 Capitol attack should be held accountable by the courts based on evidence beyond a reasonable doubt ([Alaska Dispatch News, 8/6/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:663P-1401-DYTM-N334-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   He opposed the impeachment of Donald Trump and instead called for investigations into actions by the Biden administration, including Afghanistan withdrawal and alleged ties to Ukrainian and Chinese entities ([Juneau Empire, 8/12/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6665-CB61-JD2C-J4GW-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   A letter to the editor criticized Begich for "sidestepping" the question of holding Trump directly accountable for January 6, suggesting he deferred responsibility to the courts without offering a clear position ([Alaska Dispatch News, 8/14/22](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6659-TK11-JCBJ-Y1J9-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Begich's stance underscores a general principle that no one is above the law, regardless of status or office.

-   Potential vulnerability: Critics argue that Begich avoided taking a firm stand on Trump's role in January 6, leaving some questions about his commitment to direct accountability.
