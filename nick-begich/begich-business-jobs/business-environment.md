#### Reducing Regulatory Burdens

**8/28/24: Nick Begich Supported Cutting Regulations To Accelerate Alaska Oil Projects** According to Juneau Empire (Alaska), "Both said they support cutting regulations that delay Alaska projects and oppose Democratic presidential candidate Vice President Kamala Harris' endorsements of taxing unrealized capital gains and increasing the corporate income tax rate." \[Juneau Empire (Alaska), [8/29/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CV9-B7N1-F0HF-81C1-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

**October 2024: Begich Opposed Raising Alaska Minimum Wage To \$15** According to Anchorage Daily News, "Begich said he would vote against the ballot measure, and that he was against raising Alaska s minimum wage, which has also been opposed by industry groups. 'I think what we need is to make sure that we keep entry-level jobs available for young workers and for people who need additional work. And I think that keeping a low minimum wage allows us to do that, effectively,' Begich said." \[Anchorage Daily News, [10/11/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D5D-TN41-JC89-20KW-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

**October 2024: Nick Begich Opposed Ballot Measure 1 Citing Concerns Over Minimum Wage Impacts** According to Fairbanks Daily News-Miner, "Begich plans to vote 'no,' saying that he thinks it will negatively impact Alaska's business climate and economy. And I think that keeping a low minimum wage allows us to do that,' he said." \[Fairbanks Daily News-Miner (Alaska), [10/11/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D5J-52Y1-DXVP-T0WY-00000-00&context=1519360)\]
