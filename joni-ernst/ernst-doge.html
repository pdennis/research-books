Ernst Supported DOGE And The Gutting Of The Government 

Ernst Led The DOGE Caucus 

Ernst Was To Lead The DOGE Caucus 

Ernst Was Set To Lead The Senate’s DOGE Caucus. According to the Hill, “Iowa Sen. Joni Ernst (R) is set to lead the newly created Senate DOGE Caucus — a nod to President-elect Trump’s Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) which will work outside the government to ‘dismantle Government Bureaucracy, slash excess regulations, cut wasteful expenditures, and restructure Federal Agencies,’ Trump said in a statement.” [Hill, 11/22/24]

Ernst Claimed That The DOGE Caucus Would “Fight Against The Entrenched Bureaucracy” And “Trim The Fat.” According to the Des Moines Register, “Ernst said the new Senate caucus is ready to provide oversight and ‘fight against the entrenched bureaucracy, trim the fat, and get Washington back to work for Americans.’” [Des Moines Register, 11/23/24]

Ernst Supported The Executive Unilaterally Closing Agencies 

Ernst Supported Abolishing USAID 

Ernst Joined Musk On “X Spaces” To Discuss Abolishing USAID. According to Axios, “Musk said during a late-night discussion with former DOGE co-leader Vivek Ramaswamy and Sen. Joni Ernst (R-Iowa) on his X Spaces platform he went over USAID issues in detail with Trump and checked with him a few times if he was sure that he wanted to shut it down and he agreed to do it.” [NBC News, 2/3/25]

Ernst Agreed With Musk On USAID And It Need To Be Abolished. According to NBC News, “In a post on X, Musk added that he had ‘spent the weekend feeding USAID into the wood chipper. Could gone to some great parties. Did that instead.’ Sen. Joni Ernst, R-Iowa, a longtime critic of federal spending, joined Musk on the call and said she agreed with his assessment. It’s not clear, though, how widespread that view is, even in the Republican-controlled Congress.” [NBC News, 2/3/25]

Ernst Claimed That “Pro-American” Programs Under USAID Should Be Moved To The State Department. According to Axios, “She suggested if there are ‘truly good pro-American programs, let's move them to the State Department,’ to which Musk replied: ‘Exactly.’” [NBC News, 2/3/25]
