# Congressional Campaigns

### Summary

> * Nick LaLota won the open NY-1 congressional seat in 2022, succeeding Lee Zeldin, and defeated Democrat Bridget Fleming by an 11-point margin in a district President Biden had narrowly carried in 2020 ([NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/02/21/nyregion/john-avlon-cnn-lalota.html), [Long Island Press](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CPK-X561-JBCN-354J-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Despite living in Amityville, just outside the 1st district, LaLota maintained strong local ties and was endorsed by the Suffolk County Republican Committee ([NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/06/18/nyregion/avlon-goroff-primary-ny1.html), [City & State](https://www.cityandstateny.com/politics/2022/08/lee-zeldins-congressional-district-gop-candidates-attack-each-other-insiders/375749/)).
> * LaLota successfully defended his seat in 2024, defeating well-funded Democrat John Avlon by over 10 points, and was consistently favored in polls and election forecasts ([NYT](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DBX-93T1-JBG3-634P-00000-00&context=1519360), [City & State](https://www.cityandstateny.com/politics/2024/11/republican-rep-nick-lalota-wins-reelection-defeating-former-cnn-analyst-john-avlon/400868/)).
> * LaLota positioned himself as a “common-sense conservative,” emphasized local issues, and often criticized opponents as out-of-touch or lacking district roots ([City & State](https://www.cityandstateny.com/politics/2024/11/republican-rep-nick-lalota-wins-reelection-defeating-former-cnn-analyst-john-avlon/400868/), [NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/08/19/nyregion/avlon-lalota-residency-congress.html)).
> * Democratic and outside groups targeted LaLota’s seat as a possible flip in 2024 due to the district’s swing history and his lack of seniority, but Republican-leaning trends and redistricting favored his reelection ([NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/06/18/nyregion/avlon-goroff-primary-ny1.html), [Newsday](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D91-6F21-JBKF-V1SF-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Potential vulnerability: LaLota’s residence outside the district and attacks on his opponents' residency have drawn criticism and could resurface as an issue in future races ([NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/08/19/nyregion/avlon-lalota-residency-congress.html)).
