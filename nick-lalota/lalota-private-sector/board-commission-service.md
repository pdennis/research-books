# Board and Commission Service

### Summary

> * Nick LaLota served as the Suffolk County Republican Elections Commissioner from 2015, being the youngest appointee in three decades, and was re-nominated and confirmed in 2019 ([Amityville Record](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5YDC-TKW1-DXVP-V33C-00000-00&context=1519360), [Jezebel](https://jezebel.com/gop-house-candidate-fired-a-woman-who-repeatedly-compla-1849706215)).
> 
> * During his tenure as Elections Commissioner, LaLota led a department that produced over $1.5 million in budget surpluses ([Amityville Record](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5YDC-TKW1-DXVP-V33C-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * In 2019, a Suffolk County Comptroller’s audit criticized LaLota's attendance and oversight practices, noting a lack of formal timekeeping records ([DCCC Press Release](https://dccc.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/NY-01-nick-lalota-research-book.pdf)).
> 
> * LaLota also served as chief of staff to the Suffolk County Legislature and as a trustee for the Village of Amityville, holding several leadership roles in local government since 2008 ([Suffolk County News](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6637-DSG1-DXVP-V2M5-00000-00&context=1519360), [Long Island Press](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:66M5-CGS1-JBCN-4033-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * He stepped down from the Board of Elections in December 2021 to become chief of staff for Republican Suffolk County Legislator Kevin McCaffrey, shortly before announcing his run for Congress ([DCCC Press Release](https://dccc.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/NY-01-nick-lalota-research-book.pdf)).
