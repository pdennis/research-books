# Tax Policy and Deductions

### Summary

> * Nick LaLota has made restoring or significantly raising the cap on the State and Local Tax (SALT) deduction a top priority in Congress, repeatedly introducing and co-sponsoring bills aimed at providing SALT relief for New Yorkers and Long Islanders ([Newsday](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:691S-54B1-JBKF-V01B-00000-00&context=1519360), [Rep. Nick LaLota](https://lalota.house.gov/media/press-releases/rep-nick-lalota-leads-bipartisan-call-urgent-salt-relief-smithtown-press), [CBS - 3 WREG](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FPR-J723-RRH3-W4NP-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * He has consistently worked with a bipartisan bloc of “blue state” Republicans and other SALT Caucus members to pressure House leadership and the White House, at times threatening to withhold support for broader tax legislation unless substantive SALT Cap changes are included ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/01/16/trump-tax-cuts-salt-cap-fight-ramps-up-.html), [Newsday Online](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FP7-MM53-SBM3-83CC-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * LaLota has introduced or supported various proposals—including bills to raise the cap to $15,000 (single)/$30,000 (married), alternative approaches such as means-tested deductions for lower-income households, and outright repeal of the cap ([CBS - 3 WREG](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FPR-J723-RRH3-W4NP-00000-00&context=1519360), [Herald Citizen](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:682W-TXY1-JBCN-31N1-00000-00&context=1519360), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/01/16/trump-tax-cuts-salt-cap-fight-ramps-up-.html)).
> * LaLota has publicly criticized compromise proposals—such as a $20,000 cap for joint filers—as insufficient and a “nonstarter,” demanding more substantial tax relief for high-tax regions like Long Island ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/01/16/trump-tax-cuts-salt-cap-fight-ramps-up-.html), [Newsday](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DVK-7FP3-RW49-N221-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Despite repeated legislative efforts and coordination with President Trump and other congressional allies, LaLota has not yet succeeded in enacting permanent SALT deduction cap relief—an ongoing vulnerability criticized by political opponents ([New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/10/25/nyregion/ny-voter-guide-house-races.html), [East Hampton Star](https://www.easthamptonstar.com/government/2024122/lalota-lines-behind-trump)).
