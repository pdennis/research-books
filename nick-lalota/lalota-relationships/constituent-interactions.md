# Interactions with Constituents and the Public

### Summary

> * LaLota's stance on the Trump indictment and claims of political retaliation drew strong criticism from constituents and media, with some labeling him "unfit for office" due to perceived partisanship ([Newsday, 6/14/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68FY-V7K1-JBKF-V18F-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Recurring public questions and criticisms have emerged regarding his commitment to local residency and his delayed move into the congressional district, potentially undermining his local ties ([Amityville Record, 1/18/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67BD-SHB1-JBCN-4163-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * His record has faced attacks from rivals who allege he's out of step with "suburban values" or aligned with New York City progressives ([Newsday, 8/8/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68WP-9PK1-JBKF-V49N-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Previous incidents, such as mishandled or redirected constituent communications (e.g., taking calls meant for Rep. Santos), exposed operational vulnerabilities in LaLota's office ([Newsday, 1/20/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67C2-7721-JBKF-V3PY-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Past comments responding to community criticism—including labeling opposition "xenophobic"—could be used to question his ability to engage constructively with dissenting constituents ([Newsday, 8/14/15](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5GNW-7RN1-DY2H-J48Y-00000-00&context=1519360)).
