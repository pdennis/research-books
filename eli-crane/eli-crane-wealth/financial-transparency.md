# Financial Transparency and Public Scrutiny

### Summary

-   Eli Crane repeatedly lived outside Arizona's 2nd Congressional District while running for and serving as its representative, residing in Oro Valley or Tucson, which are in the 6th District ([Cronkite News](https://cronkitenews.azpbs.org/2022/10/14/us-house-district-2-eli-crane/), [Arizona Republic](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BVH-C021-DXGX-607X-00000-00&context=1519360), [Arizona Mirror](https://azmirror.com/briefs/eli-crane-wins-second-term-in-u-s-house-of-representatives/)).

-   Arizona law does not require congressional candidates or members to live within the district they represent, only within the state ([Cronkite News](https://cronkitenews.azpbs.org/2022/10/14/us-house-district-2-eli-crane/), [Arizona Capitol Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:665S-2GS1-F053-W28W-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Crane's residency outside his district has drawn ongoing public scrutiny, criticism from political opponents, and questions from constituents, including letters to editors and campaign opponents ([Arizona Republic](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CS9-V271-DXGX-635H-00000-00&context=1519360), [Arizona Daily Star](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C35-G1R1-DY0W-42V3-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   The Pima County Recorder's Office confirmed Crane's registered voter address as being outside the 2nd District ([Arizona Republic](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BVH-C021-DXGX-607X-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Crane faced further public scrutiny for hiring a fundraising consultant with a prior drug conviction and did not respond to questions regarding this decision ([Arizona Mirror](https://azmirror.com/briefs/one-of-eli-cranes-fundraising-consultants-was-busted-for-a-meth-lab-in-2016/)).
