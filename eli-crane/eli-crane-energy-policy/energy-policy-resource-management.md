# Energy Policy and Resource Management

### Summary

-   Rep. Eli Crane consistently opposed Biden administration actions restricting energy development, including bans on uranium mining near the Grand Canyon and buffer zones near Chaco Canyon, calling them harmful to local economies and tribal sovereignty ([Navajo Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F34-TMY3-RVND-R3KJ-00000-00), [Arizona Republic](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D1B-W5M1-JB1V-G1W7-00000-00)).

-   Crane introduced and supported legislation such as the Energy Opportunities for All Act to nullify federal bans on resource development and advocated that tribal nations control their own land and resources ([Press Release - Rep. Eli Crane](https://crane.house.gov/2025/01/28/crane-reintroduces-bill-to-nullify-biden-admins-obstructive-violation-of-tribal-sovereignty/), [Navajo Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F34-TMY3-RVND-R3KJ-00000-00)).

-   He aligned with Republican efforts to prevent federal restrictions on household energy choices, such as voting for the Gas Stove Protection and Freedom Act ([Cronkite News: Arizona State University](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68G4-T8W1-DXVP-T1TR-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Crane championed water rights and infrastructure for tribal communities, supporting the Navajo water rights settlement, securing EPA waivers for critical water projects, and highlighting Israeli water technology's potential benefits for the Southwest ([Navajo Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FBG-HS53-S2FH-M281-00000-00), [Arizona Republic](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68DP-MJK1-JB1V-G1HY-00000-00)).

-   Potential vulnerabilities include criticism from local advocates and Native community members who argue that Crane's opposition to public land protections and uranium mining bans threatens environmental and water safety ([Arizona Daily Sun](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:694T-1TS1-JBCN-3226-00000-00)).

### Fossil Fuels and Resource Development

#### Stance on regulatory barriers for extraction

**2025: Eli Crane Reintroduced Energy Opportunities for All Act to Nullify Biden Admin Policy** According to a press release from Rep. Eli Crane's office, "Rep. Eli Crane (R-AZ) reintroduced the Energy Opportunities for All Act, which would nullify the Biden admin's overreaching actions that ban American citizens from lawfully exercising the rights over their lands and resources." \[Press Release - Rep. Eli Crane, [1/28/25](https://crane.house.gov/2025/01/28/crane-reintroduces-bill-to-nullify-biden-admins-obstructive-violation-of-tribal-sovereignty/)\]

**June 2023: Eli Crane Voted With Republicans To Advance Gas Stove Legislation** According to Cronkite News: Arizona State University, "But all 11 voted in line with fellow Republicans Tuesday, clearing the way for more votes, including the Gas Stove Protection and Freedom Act that prevents the federal government from implementing new restrictions on such stoves." \[Cronkite News: Arizona State University (Tempe), [6/14/23](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:68G4-T8W1-DXVP-T1TR-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

**Eli Crane Opposed Grand Canyon National Monument's Uranium Mining Ban** According to Arizona Republic, "the two are on opposite sides of President Biden's decision to establish the Baaj Nwaavjo I'tah Kukveni - Ancestral Footprints of the Grand Canyon National Monument. Nez views the accompanying ban on new uranimum mining as protection of tribes' ancestral home; Crane dismisses it as pandering to the far-left at the expense of ore production that's critical for national security." \[Arizona Republic (Phoenix), [9/22/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D1B-W5M1-JB1V-G1W7-00000-00&context=1519360)\]

This uranium will be transported through Flagstaff and across the Navajo Nation in ill-equipped vehicles." \[Letter to the Editor - Arizona Daily Sun, [9/29/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D2W-TMT1-DXVP-T1PT-00000-00&context=1519360)\]
