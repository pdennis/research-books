# Overall Health Care Policy Stances and Ideology

### Summary

-   Eli Crane has repeatedly questioned COVID-19 vaccine efficacy, called vaccine outreach "totalitarian," and cited far-right anti-vaccine influencer Candace Owens as an inspiration ([DCCC Research Memo](https://dccc.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/220906-Eli-Crane-AZ-02-Research-Memo-ONLINE-FINAL.pdf)).

-   He co-sponsored legislation to withdraw the U.S. from the World Health Organization (WHO), calling the agency a "corrupt pawn of Communist China" and urging supporters to help end federal pandemic mandates ([Arizona Daily Star](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67WR-SMX1-JBCN-344J-00000-00&context=1519360), [Arizona Republic](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:67WJ-PFS1-JB1V-G0RT-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Crane voted against federal efforts to end COVID-19 public health emergencies and has focused heavily on investigating the U.S. government's COVID-19 response, including actions taken by Anthony Fauci ([AFL-CIO scorecard](https://aflcio.org/scorecard/legislators/eli-crane), [Arizona Republic](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6857-N261-JB1V-G2FF-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   He was named to a GOP-led committee probing Arizona's COVID-19 policies, signaling a priority on reviewing and critiquing pandemic management ([Arizona Republic](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6857-N261-JB1V-G2FF-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Potential vulnerability: Crane has faced criticism, including from constituents, for allegedly supporting "anti-healthcare laws" that could negatively impact women ([Arizona Daily Sun](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BG4-3HS1-DXVP-T4FH-00000-00&context=1519360)).

### Stance on Public Health Institutions and COVID-19

#### Views on Federal COVID-19 Policies and Restrictions

**Eli Crane Questioned COVID-19 Vaccines and Cited Anti-Vaccine Influencer** According to a press release from the DCCC Research Department, "Crane said the Biden administration's COVID-19 vaccine awareness canvassing was 'totalitarian' and suggested that the COVID-19 vaccine was 'not even a vaccine.' \[...\] Crane said that Candace Owens, a far-right influencer, was one of his influences and 'one of the most courageous people in this country.'" \[Press Release - DCCC Research Memo, [9/8/22](https://dccc.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/220906-Eli-Crane-AZ-02-Research-Memo-ONLINE-FINAL.pdf)\]
