# Campaign Finance and Ethics

### Summary

> * Rep. Andy Ogles admitted in May 2024 that a reported $320,000 personal loan to his campaign never occurred and amended his campaign finance filings accordingly, describing the amount as a “pledge” rather than an actual loan ([Nashville Post](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C3T-H9H1-DXVP-T0BR-00000-00&context=1519360), [Leaf-Chronicle](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C64-2DJ1-JB1V-G4XM-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Ogles is the subject of an FBI probe and House ethics scrutiny related to errors in his campaign finance filings, particularly the inaccurate report of the $320,000 self-loan; federal agents confiscated his cell phone as part of the investigation ([Oak Ridger](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F0F-B393-SJ5R-H0BR-00000-00&context=1519360), [Jackson Sun](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F0F-B393-SJ5R-H0YC-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * The Office of Congressional Ethics formally advised the U.S. House Ethics Committee in January 2025 to launch a full investigation into Ogles’ campaign finance practices ([Oak Ridger](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F0F-B393-SJ5R-H0BR-00000-00&context=1519360), [Jackson Sun](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F0F-B393-SJ5R-H0YC-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Ogles has faced repeated FEC scrutiny, fines, and complaints for campaign finance disclosure errors since 2022, including receiving excessive donations, discrepancies in reporting, missing required information, and an ethics complaint alleging a “pattern of malfeasance” ([Columbia Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:694Y-VXT1-DXGX-60F1-00000-00&context=1519360), [Tennessean](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DVC-X583-SGJK-91P9-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Ogles’ campaign and related PACs shared the same treasurer, and his former campaign treasurer became the sole donor to a super PAC that supported his candidacy, raising questions about potential coordination and ethical practices ([Knoxville News-Sentinel](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CKD-H061-DXGX-61MM-00000-00&context=1519360), [Daily News Journal](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CKM-G1S1-JB1V-G2CJ-00000-00&context=1519360)).
