# Legislative Actions on Disaster Management

### Summary

> * In October 2024, Andy Ogles introduced legislation seeking to add $10 billion in disaster relief funding for FEMA by redirecting funds originally allocated for IRS agents in the 2022 federal budget ([Chattanooga Times Free Press](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DBJ-S2K1-DY58-F046-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * During the COVID-19 pandemic, Ogles coordinated with local and state leaders, and issued a state of emergency in Maury County in March 2020, enabling access to emergency funds from state and federal agencies ([Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5YGY-FP21-DXVP-V16S-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Ogles emphasized cooperation and communication with local agencies and health organizations to facilitate emergency response efforts and decision-making during crises such as the pandemic and severe weather events ([Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5YFF-NB21-DXVP-V39P-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * He has publicly commended both city and county leaderships for proactive disaster responses, highlighting coordinated actions across multiple municipalities ([Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5YGY-FP21-DXVP-V16S-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Potential vulnerability: The proposal to reallocate IRS funding to disaster relief may face political and operational challenges, particularly regarding the impact on tax enforcement and revenue collection ([Chattanooga Times Free Press](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DBJ-S2K1-DY58-F046-00000-00&context=1519360)).
