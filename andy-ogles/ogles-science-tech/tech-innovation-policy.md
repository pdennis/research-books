# Positions on Technology and Innovation Policy

### Summary

> * Andy Ogles has repeatedly used taxpayer funds for digital advertising campaigns, mass emails, and text messages highlighting his legislative record, with at least 15 such requests approved as "frankable" by the bipartisan House Communications Standards Commission since February 2024 ([Leaf-Chronicle](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BYP-XX81-DXGX-60TS-00000-00&context=1519360); [Jackson Sun](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BYP-XX81-DXGX-61FR-00000-00&context=1519360); [Commercial Appeal](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BYP-XX81-DXGX-6321-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Ogles has openly supported technology sector growth, describing a General Motors investment in Maury County as positioning the region and Tennessee at the forefront of electric vehicle innovation ([Tennessean](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6147-31P1-DYJJ-P2P0-00000-00&context=1519360); [Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6146-F661-JBCN-44VP-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * In early 2021, Ogles strongly criticized major technology companies like Twitter, Facebook, Amazon, Apple, and Google for banning President Trump and accused them of targeting conservatives and Trump supporters, claiming such actions amount to censorship ([Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:61SB-43H1-JBCN-41C8-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Following the removal of President Trump from social media platforms, Ogles called for Tennessee state officials to divest any holdings in large tech companies and advocated for a boycott of their services until what he termed "censorship and prohibitions" ended ([Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:61SB-43H1-JBCN-41C8-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Ogles argued that the blocking and removal of Parler, a social media app popular with conservatives, was a deliberate effort to stifle competition in the marketplace ([Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:61SB-43H1-JBCN-41C8-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Potential vulnerability: Ogles' use of taxpayer funds for self-promotional digital ads—even though these were approved under official rules—may attract scrutiny or criticism over the appropriateness of using public money for this type of communication ([Commercial Appeal](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6BYP-XX81-DXGX-6321-00000-00&context=1519360)).
