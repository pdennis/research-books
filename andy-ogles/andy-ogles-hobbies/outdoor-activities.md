# Outdoor and Recreational Activities

### Summary

> * Andy Ogles played a major role in organizing large public events in Maury County, such as MuleFest in 2021, which drew over 30,000 attendees, and 9/11 memorial services in 2020 that saw more than 3,000 participants ([Tennessean](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6301-GY31-DYJJ-P4GM-00000-00&context=1519360), [Daily Herald](https://www.columbiadailyherald.com/story/news/local/columbia/2020/09/16/more-than-3-000-attend-9-11-memorial-columbia/5805815002/)).
> 
> * Ogles was a vocal supporter of continuing local festivals and fairs during the COVID-19 pandemic, advocating for events like the Maury County Fair, which saw record attendance under his leadership despite public health concerns ([Daily Herald](https://www.columbiadailyherald.com/story/news/local/columbia/2020/09/16/more-than-3-000-attend-9-11-memorial-columbia/5805815002/), [Tennessean](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6301-GY31-DYJJ-P4GM-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * He helped promote tourism and economic recovery through community events, emphasizing Maury County as a destination for families and supporting partnerships with local businesses and organizations ([Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:62X3-XMB1-JBCN-42XW-00000-00&context=1519360), [Tennessean](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:63BG-2Y31-DYJJ-P188-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> 
> * Ogles engaged with recreational and animal welfare initiatives, such as introducing trout to Woodland Park Pond and responding promptly to animal welfare concerns at MuleFest, including arranging veterinary checks ([Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5Y13-H8C1-DXVP-V08M-00000-00&context=1519360), [Columbia Daily Herald](https://www.columbiadailyherald.com/story/news/local/columbia/2021/06/03/authorities-investigate-concerns-horse-seen-mulefest/7497666002/)).
> 
> * As mayor, he credited improvements at the Maury County Animal Shelter to strong leadership and staff training, leading to lower euthanasia rates and better animal care ([Daily Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:5XB9-XMS1-DXVP-V47N-00000-00&context=1519360)).
