# Endorsements and Support from Advocacy Groups

### Summary

> * Andy Ogles received significant backing from Americans for Prosperity Action (AFP Action), which spent over $177,000 on ad buys, mailers, and door-to-door outreach supporting his campaign in 2024 ([Tennessean, 7/22/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CJ4-NP61-DXGX-6050-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * AFP Action organized grassroots activities for Ogles, including a campaign kickoff event he attended and a "Day of Action" that saw canvassers paid to knock on 3,500 doors; overall, AFP Action coordinated closely with his campaign ([Tennessean, 7/3/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CD3-77N1-JB1V-G034-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Less than 1% of AFP Action’s funding came from Tennessee donors, with the majority sourced from national interests—primarily billionaire Charles Koch—potentially raising questions about out-of-state influence ([Tennessean, 7/22/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CJ4-NP61-DXGX-6050-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Ogles was endorsed in July 2024 by Competitive Markets Action for his advocacy on competitive markets and agricultural transparency, receiving praise from the organization’s leadership ([Knoxville News-Sentinel, 7/28/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CKD-H061-DXGX-61MM-00000-00&context=1519360)).
> * Additional national super PACs, such as Club for Growth PAC, House Freedom Action, and Gun Rights America, also contributed to Ogles’ campaign, bringing total outside support close to $225,000 ([Tennessean, 7/22/24](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CJ4-NP61-DXGX-6050-00000-00&context=1519360)).
