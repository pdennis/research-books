# National Nuclear Security Administration

### Funding

**2022: Schweikert Voted Against Providing \$20.7 Billion To The National Nuclear Security Administration.** In March 2022, according to Congressional Quarterly, Schweikert voted against the second portion of the Fiscal 2022 Omnibus Appropriations, which would provide "\$20.7 billion for the National Nuclear Security Administration." The vote was on the motion to concur in the Senate amendment with a House amendment. The bill was divided and this vote was the second portion of the bill. The House concurred with the Senate by a vote of 260-171. After resolving differences, the bill was sent to the President and became law. \[House Vote 66, [3/9/22](http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2022/roll066.xml); Congressional Quarterly, [3/9/22](https://plus.cq.com/vote/2022/H/66?8); Congressional Actions, [H.R. 2471](https://www.congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/house-bill/2471/actions)\]
