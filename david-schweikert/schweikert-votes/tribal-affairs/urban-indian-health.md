# Urban Indian Health Care

**2021: Schweikert Voted For Ensuring That The Indian Health Service And Other Offices Communicate Thoroughly With Urban Indian Organizations To Enact Provisions Of Law Regarding Health Care For Native Americans And Alaska Natives In Urban Areas.** In November 2021, Schweikert voted for the Urban Indian Health Confer Act which would, according to Congressional Quarterly, "require the Health and Human Services Department to ensure that the Indian Health Service and other department offices confer to the maximum extent practicable with urban Indian organizations in carrying out provisions of law relating to health care for Native Americans and Alaska Natives living in urban areas." The vote was on passage. The House passed the bill by a vote of 406-17. \[House Vote 343, [11/2/21](http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2021/roll343.xml); Congressional Quarterly, [11/2/21](https://plus.cq.com/vote/2021/H/343?19); Congressional Actions, [H.R. 5221](https://www.congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/house-bill/5221/actions)\]
