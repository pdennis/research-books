# Military Commissions

### Online Proceedings

**2022: Schweikert Voted Against An Amendment That Required Military Commission Proceedings To Be Made Publicly Available Online.** In July 2022, according to Congressional Quarterly, Schweikert voted against an amendment to the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2023, which would "require proceedings for military commissions to be publicly available on the internet." The vote was on the adoption of an amendment. The House adopted the amendment by a vote 218-207. \[House Vote 310, [7/13/22](http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2022/roll310.xml); Congressional Quarterly, [7/13/22](https://plus.cq.com/vote/2022/H/310?4); Congressional Actions, [H.Amdt. 246](https://www.congress.gov/amendment/117th-congress/house-amendment/246/actions?r=46&s=a); Congressional Actions, [H.R. 7900](https://www.congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/house-bill/7900/actions)\]
