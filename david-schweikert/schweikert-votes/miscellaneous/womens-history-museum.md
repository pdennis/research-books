# National Women's History Museum

### Creating A Commission To Study Potential Creation Of A National Women's History Museum

**2014: Schweikert Voted To Create A Commission To Study The Potential Creation Of A National Women's History Museum.** In May 2014, Schweikert voted for legislation that would have, according to Congressional Quarterly, "create\[d\] a commission to study the possible establishment and maintenance of a National Women\'s History Museum in Washington, D.C." The vote was on a motion to suspend the rules and pass the bill. The House agreed to the motion and passed the bill by a vote of 383 to 33. The Senate took no substantive action on the legislation. \[House Vote 201, [5/7/14](http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2014/roll201.xml); Congressional Quarterly, [5/7/14](http://cq.com/vote/2014/H/201?0); Congressional Actions, [H.R. 863](https://www.congress.gov/bill/113th-congress/house-bill/863/all-actions)\]
