# Parole Authority

### Family Stability

**2024: Schweikert Voted To Prohibit Funding For A Family Stability Parole Process.** In June 2024, Schweikert voted for according to Congressional Quarterly, "amendment no. 54 that would prohibit the use of funds to carry out, enforce or implement the 2024 Homeland Security Department process titled, 'Process to Promote the Unity and Stability of Families.'" The vote was on the amendment. The underlying legislation was the FY 2025 Homeland Security Appropriations. The House rejected the amendment by a vote of 193 to 216. \[House Vote 293, [6/26/24](http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2024/roll293.xml); Congressional Quarterly, [6/26/24](https://plus.cq.com/vote/2024/H/293?11); Congressional Actions, [H.Amdt.1023](https://www.congress.gov/amendment/118th-congress/house-amendment/1023/actions?s=a&r=29); Congressional Actions, [H.R. 8752](http://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/house-bill/8752/all-actions)\]

### Funding

**2024: Schweikert Voted To Prohibit Funding For The Cuban, Haitian, Nicaraguan, And Venezuelan Parole Program.** In June 2024, Schweikert voted for , according to Congressional Quarterly, "amendment no. 20 that would prohibit the use of funds in the bill for the parole program titled 'Processes for Cubans, Haitians, Nicaraguans and Venezuelans.'" The vote was on the amendment. The underlying legislation was the FY 2025 Homeland Security Appropriations. The House rejected the amendment by a vote of 193 to 218. \[House Vote 288, [6/26/24](http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2024/roll288.xml); Congressional Quarterly, [6/26/24](https://plus.cq.com/vote/2024/H/288?11); Congressional Actions, [H.Amdt.1008](https://www.congress.gov/amendment/118th-congress/house-amendment/1008/actions?s=a&r=29); Congressional Actions, [H.R. 8752](http://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/house-bill/8752/all-actions)\]

### Limit To Case-By-Case Basis

**2023: Schweikert Voted To Limit Parole Authority That Allows Non-Citizens To Temporarily Live And Work In The U.S. To Only A Case-By-Case Basis.** In May 2023, according to Congressional Quarterly, Schweikert voted for the Secure The Border Act, which would "limit 'parole' authority allowing noncitizens to temporarily live and work in the U.S. allowing DHS to grant parole only on a case-by-case individual basis." The vote was on passage. The House passed the bill by a vote of 219 to 213, thus the bill was sent to the Senate. \[House Vote 209, [5/11/23](http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2023/roll209.xml); Congressional Quarterly, [5/11/23](https://plus.cq.com/vote/2023/H/209?5); Congressional Actions, [H.R. 2](https://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/house-bill/2/all-actions)\]

-   **The Bill Would Resume The Border Wall Construction, Raise Funding For Border Agents And Upgrade Border Equipment, Re-Implement The "Remain In Mexico Policy," Create New Restrictions On Asylum-Seeking, And Enhance E-Verify.** According to CNN, "The bill would restart construction of a border wall, increase funding for border agents and upgraded border technology, reinstate the 'remain in Mexico' policy, place new restrictions on asylum seekers, and enhance requirements for E-verify, a database employers use to verify immigration status." \[CNN, [5/11/23](https://www.cnn.com/2023/05/11/politics/house-gop-border-bill-vote/index.html)\]
