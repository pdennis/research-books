# Relationships with Political Individuals and Parties

### Summary

-   Jeff Hurd faced significant hostility and lack of support from Colorado GOP leadership, including public criticism, withheld funding, and the state party actively endorsing other candidates ([Colorado Politics](https://www.coloradopolitics.com/capitol-s-new-crop-jeff-hurd-colorado-s-accidental-politician-who-is-taking-over-boebert/article_8c96aa5b-6bc2-55cf-909a-8a5e21e37e3e.html), [Denver Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DJP-M7G1-JBCN-34CV-00000-00&context=1519360))

-   Hurd's ambiguous stance and repeated refusal to disclose whether he supported or voted for Donald Trump resulted in attacks from both Republican rivals and party activists, raising doubts about his commitment to the GOP base ([Colorado Sun](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D9M-4031-JBCN-323C-00000-00&context=1519360), [Colorado Politics](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6C1K-1YP1-DXVP-T0ND-00000-00&context=1519360))

-   Mailers and public statements during the campaign questioned Hurd's conservative credentials, particularly on key issues like immigration and law enforcement, portraying him as insufficiently supportive of Trump's policies ([Durango Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D9P-5MV1-JBCN-340K-00000-00&context=1519360), [Colorado Sun](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D9M-4031-JBCN-323C-00000-00&context=1519360))

-   Several in the Republican base and leadership accused Hurd of being an establishment or "RINO" (Republican in Name Only), leading to questions about his ability to rally conservatives and build a unified party coalition ([Coloradopolitics.com](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6B2C-74K1-DXVP-T073-00000-00&context=1519360), [Montrose Daily Press](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6CMX-5BK1-DXVP-T04N-00000-00&context=1519360))

-   Hurd's ability to work with other prominent Republicans, such as Lauren Boebert, was at times questioned due to differences in governing style, potentially complicating unified representation for the district ([Durango Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D3R-PGC1-JBCN-311G-00000-00&context=1519360), [Denver Gazette](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D3H-85M1-DXVP-T0D8-00000-00&context=1519360))

-   Hurd's criticism of Trump---especially on issues like the January 6 pardons---risked alienating the MAGA-aligned portion of the GOP, a key constituency in Republican primaries ([Durango Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FHM-7TB3-S9PX-J23X-00000-00&context=1519360), [Vail Daily](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FCY-3G43-RRKP-K4SK-00000-00&context=1519360))
