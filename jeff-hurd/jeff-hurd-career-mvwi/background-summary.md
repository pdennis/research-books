# Professional and Personal Background

### Summary

-   Jeff Hurd is a Grand Junction-based attorney who became a first-time candidate and won election to the U.S. House of Representatives for Colorado's 3rd Congressional District in 2024, succeeding Lauren Boebert ([Colorado Politics](https://www.coloradopolitics.com/elections/2024/republican-jeff-hurd-endorsed-by-colorado-legislators/article_4aa2f312-a5a9-11ee-9799-0f10542c49ca.html); [Durango Herald](https://www.durangoherald.com/articles/u-s-rep-jeff-hurd-talks-doge-energy-policy-ukraine-in-first-tele-town-hall/)).

-   Prior to his congressional career, Hurd was chief counsel for the Delta-Montrose Electric Association and was noted for his legal experience and community involvement in western Colorado ([Steamboat Pilot & Today](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FDT-5B63-RRST-G33K-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Hurd is a Colorado native, raised and residing in Grand Junction with his family, identifying himself as a "citizen legislator" rather than a career politician ([Alamosa News](https://www.alamosanews.com/stories/a-conversation-with-cd3-candidate-jeff-hurd,50233); [Journal-Advocate](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FC2-77J3-RSD7-B3WJ-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Upon taking office in January 2025, Hurd was assigned to three key House committees: Natural Resources; Transportation and Infrastructure; and Science, Space, and Technology ([Montrose Daily Press](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DS3-BNF3-RRTG-J1T8-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Hurd is generally described as a mainstream Republican with traditional and "constitutional conservative" values, emphasizing relationship-building, pragmatism, and effective constituent service ([Fort Morgan Times](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FM2-M0M3-RWPF-44M0-00000-00&context=1519360); [Colorado Politics](https://www.coloradopolitics.com/q-and-a/q-a-with-us-rep-elect-jeff-hurd-colorado-republican-navigates-dc-prepares-to-take/article_29b7f154-a867-11ef-9aab-c7a71a5b99c7.html)).

-   Some criticisms and potential vulnerabilities include perceptions of low campaign visibility, limited in-person engagement with constituents, and allegations of unresponsiveness from his office and staff ([Durango Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6D9P-5MV1-JBCN-340F-00000-00&context=1519360); [Montrose Daily Press](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FHM-7TB3-S9PX-J44P-00000-00&context=1519360)).
