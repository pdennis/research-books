# Legislative Activities and Policy Positions

### Summary

-   Jeff Hurd prioritized policy areas including natural resources, federal lands, energy, agriculture, border security, water, and cost of living issues, reflecting both his district's needs and his professional background ([The Daily Sentinel](https://www.gjsentinel.com/news/western_colorado/q-a-u-s-house-rep-jeff-hurd-talks-early-days-in-congress/article_e380af5e-e25f-11ef-a710-0bd3c4a60687.html), [CPR News](https://www.cpr.org/2024/06/03/vg-2024-colorado-primary-elections-congressional-district-3-candidate-jeff-hurd/), [Denver Post](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6DC5-CV71-JBCN-319D-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   He chaired the Indian and Insular Affairs Subcommittee in the House Natural Resources Committee and served on multiple committees, including Science, Space and Technology, and Transportation and Infrastructure ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeff_Hurd_(politician)), [Montrose Daily Press](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F0T-YPV3-SDMJ-Y0PP-00000-00&context=1519360)).

-   Hurd introduced and supported legislation to relocate the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) headquarters to Grand Junction, advocated for overturning Biden-era public lands management plans, sponsored the Gold King Mine spill compensation bill, and pushed for bipartisan limits on presidential tariff authority ([Durango Herald](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F9B-MM03-RRTY-W065-00000-00&context=1519360), [Denver Post](https://www.denverpost.com/2025/03/12/colorado-jeff-hurd-drilling-public-lands-energy-blm-congress/), [Colorado Politics](https://www.coloradopolitics.com/colorado-in-dc/colorado-republican-jeff-hurd-leads-bipartisan-legislation-to-check-trumps-power-to-impose-tariffs/article_c797684b-ebf1-444f-b9ce-f90624ff58c6.html)).

-   He positioned himself as a "constitutional conservative" with a focus on pragmatic, principled lawmaking, and expressed a willingness to work across the aisle through bipartisan caucuses like the Problem Solvers Caucus ([CPR News](https://www.cpr.org/2024/10/14/vg-2024-3rd-congressional-district-jeff-hurd/), [Colorado Politics](https://www.coloradopolitics.com/colorado-in-dc/colorado-republican-jeff-hurd-leads-bipartisan-legislation-to-check-trumps-power-to-impose-tariffs/article_c797684b-ebf1-444f-b9ce-f90624ff58c6.html)).

-   Hurd supported government spending cuts, including Medicaid reforms tied to work requirements, while stating a commitment to protecting vulnerable recipients and ensuring equitable Medicaid allocations for Colorado ([Sopris Sun](https://soprissun.com/congressman-hurd-votes-to-cut-government-spending-tells-constituents-he-supports-medicaid/)).

-   Potential vulnerabilities include criticism for lack of detailed policy positions, reliance on constitutional rhetoric without specifics, and challenges regarding his stances on federal workforce reductions ([Durango Herald letter](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6F7R-GH73-RRT0-R4WC-00000-00&context=1519360), [Aspen Times letter](https://advance.lexis.com/api/document?collection=news&id=urn:contentItem:6FJ4-WDC3-RRX6-53PJ-00000-00&context=1519360)).
